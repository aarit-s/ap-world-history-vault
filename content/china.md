tang dynasty (618-907 CE) -- china's "golden age" before the song:

- chinese viewed themselves as the MIDDLE KINGDOM -- positioned neighboring states as inferior
- enforced tributary system requiring the kowtow (ritual bow) to acknowledge chinese supremacy
- [[korea]], [[japan]], [[vietnam]] were all tributary states
- innovations:
  - improved irrigation -> agricultural productivity boom
  - advanced steel + metalwork
  - invented gunpowder (initially for fireworks/medicine -- weaponized later)
  - woodblock printing -- spread literacy + buddhist texts
  - "flying cash" -- early paper money / credit system
- [[buddhism]] expansion under tang:
  - monk xuanzang traveled to [[india]], returned with buddhist texts
  - translation efforts -> growth of monasteries
  - zen (chan) [[buddhism]] emerged through [[syncretism]] -- blending buddhism with [[daoism]]
  - BUT: wealthy monasteries gained tax-exempt status, threatening state revenue
  - tang eventually confiscated monastic lands + shut down many monasteries

song dynasty:  
How did the song dynasty maintain and justify its rule?
1. Confusciansm
  1. NEO confusciansm -- get rid of buddhist [[buddhism]] influence
    1. championed by ZHU XI -- combined classic [[confucianism]] morality with buddhist metaphysics
  2. The nature of society is hierarchical (everyone has a place)
  3. In order to acheive harmony: those below listen to above, those above listen to below
  4. HUGE EMPHASIS ON ROLE OF ELDERS:
    1. Filial Piety -- you should always do something that would probably make your elders proud
    2. FIVE RELATIONSHIPS: ruler-subject, father-son, husband-wife, elder-younger, friend-friend
  5. Women were SUBOORDINATES
    1. Stripped of legal rights (could only inherit property if NO male heirs existed)
    2. Lots of pain
    3. Limited access to education -- elite girls learned calligraphy + poetry at home, formal schools were male-only
    4. Foot Binding
      1. Status symbol -- bound feet = elite status + reduced mobility = domestic confinement
2. Expansion of empirial buereaucracy:
  1. What is a buereaucracy
    1. Government entity arranged in a hierachical fashion that carries out the will of the emporer
  2. Civil State examination
    1. Tested on the FOUR BOOKS + FIVE CLASSICS (confucian texts)
    2. Three exam levels: PROVINCIAL -> METROPOLITAN -> PALACE
    3. Theoretically open to all but you needed years of study = de facto educated elite (the SHI class / scholar-gentry)
    4. Created the unified TANG-SONG CODE (legal system)
- Neighboring regions followed suit:
  - [[korea]] -- adopted exam system, created the YANGBAN scholar-officer class
  - [[japan]] -- imported confucian admin during heian -> kamakura periods
  - [[vietnam]] (đại việt) -- modeled bureaucracy on chinese institutions

song deeper detail:

- controlled less territory than tang but achieved GREATER economic prosperity + tech
- scholar-gentry class -- educated elites who staffed bureaucracy, replaced old hereditary aristocracy
  - major AP concept: meritocracy vs aristocracy
- proto-industrialization -- mass production + sophisticated trade networks
- cities like hangzhou + chang'an reached MILLIONS in population
- government ended forced labor on public projects -> transitioned to wage labor (stimulated economic growth)
- maritime tech: improvements to magnetic compass, stern-post rudder, junk ships
  - these revolutionized maritime trade
  - later enabled zheng he voyages in ming dynasty
