[[unit 5]] -- revolutions:

- intellectual cause: [[enlightenment]]
- [[revoultions]]:
  - american (1776)
  - french (1789)
  - haitian (1791-1804) -- only successful slave revolt
  - latin american (bolivar, san martin)
- political: liberalism, nationalism, socialism, marxism
- economic: [[industrial revolution]] starts in britain, spreads
- abolition of slavery + serfdom
- women's rights movement begins (seneca falls 1848)

[[unit 6]] -- consequences of industrialization:

- [[imperialism]] full speed ahead
- [[scramble for africa]]
- economic imperialism in [[china]] ([[opium wars]]) + [[ottoman]]
- indigenous responses:
  - rebellion ([[sepoy mutiny]], [[boxer rebellion]])
  - modernization ([[tanzimat]], [[self strengthening]], [[meiji restoration]])
- [[migration]] explodes -- 50m+ europeans to americas, plus indentured indian/chinese labor
- nativism backlash
