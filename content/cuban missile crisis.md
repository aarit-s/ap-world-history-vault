[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]  
oct 1962 -- 13-day standoff between us and ussr that almost ended the world

setup:

- 1959 -- fidel castro takes over cuba, becomes communist, allies with ussr
- cuba is 90 miles from florida -- huge security problem for us
- 1961 -- us tries + fails to overthrow castro at BAY OF PIGS (massive embarrassment)
- 1962 -- us deploys jupiter nuclear missiles in turkey (right next to ussr)
- ussr response: secretly install nuclear missiles in cuba (right next to us)

the crisis:

- us spy planes (u-2) photograph soviet missile sites being built in cuba
- JFK announces a NAVAL BLOCKADE ("quarantine") of cuba -- if soviet ships try to deliver more missiles, war
- 13 days of negotiation, soviet ships turn around at the last minute
- khrushchev's offer: ussr removes cuban missiles if us promises to never invade cuba (PUBLIC deal)
- secret backchannel: us also removes jupiter missiles from turkey (KEPT SECRET so jfk didnt look weak)

aftermath:

- closest the world ever came to nuclear war -- this is the [[mutually assured destruction]] scenario almost activating
- led to MOSCOW-WASHINGTON HOTLINE (red phone) so leaders could call each other directly
- led to nuclear non-proliferation treaty 1968
- khrushchev gets ousted by soviet politburo 1964 (seen as having lost)
- jfk's reputation soars -- though he gets assassinated 1963

big picture:

- textbook example of how [[cold war]] tension could have ended in actual nuclear annihilation
- demonstrated that proxies + brinksmanship had hard limits
- both sides realized they needed COMMUNICATION mechanisms to avoid accidental armageddon
- classic AP example for "cold war crises" + "global interconnection of conflict"
