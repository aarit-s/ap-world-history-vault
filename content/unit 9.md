[[1900-present]]
globalization (1991-present)

post-cold war world:
	us = lone superpower (briefly)
	"end of history" thesis (francis fukuyama) -- liberal democracy + capitalism won
	this turns out to be very wrong long-term

GLOBALIZATION:
	free trade agreements -- [[nafta]] 1994, WTO 1995, EU expansion
	multinational corporations dominate global economy
	supply chains span continents (apple, walmart, amazon)
	global labor market -- jobs move to wherever labor is cheapest
	cultural globalization -- hollywood, mcdonalds, kpop, soccer
	english as global lingua franca

DIGITAL REVOLUTION:
	internet goes mainstream 1990s
	dot-com boom 1995-2000
	smartphones (iphone 2007)
	social media (facebook 2004, twitter 2006, tiktok 2016)
	transformed every industry + politics
	[[arab spring]] used social media as organizing tool

[[middle east modern]] + [[war on terror]]:
	9/11 2001 -> us invades afghanistan + iraq
	osama bin laden killed 2011
	rise of isis 2014
	syrian civil war 2011-present
	[[arab spring]] 2010-12 -- mostly failed but transformed politics

RISE OF CHINA:
	deng xiaoping reforms (started in unit 8) pay off
	china becomes world's factory
	by 2010 = 2nd largest economy
	xi jinping consolidates power 2012+
	belt + road initiative -- chinese infrastructure investment globally
	tensions with us over trade, taiwan, south china sea

OTHER RISING POWERS (BRICS):
	brazil, russia, india, china, south africa
	challenging us-led world order

CLIMATE CHANGE:
	scientific consensus -- humans causing global warming via co2 emissions
	kyoto protocol 1997, paris agreement 2015
	already happening: extreme weather, sea level rise, fires, droughts
	political polarization slowing response

PANDEMICS:
	hiv/aids 1980s-present (esp africa)
	covid-19 2020-2022 -- first global pandemic since 1918 spanish flu
		exposed supply chain weaknesses
		mass remote work transition
		~7 million dead worldwide

CURRENT/RECENT:
	russia invades ukraine 2022 (largest european war since wwii)
	gaza war 2023-present
	ai revolution -- chatgpt 2022, transforming work + education
	rise of authoritarianism (orban, putin, modi-style nationalism, trump)
	demographic crisis -- aging populations in europe, japan, china

big picture:
	world more connected than ever -- but also fragmenting along new lines
	nation-states still dominant but multinational corporations + non-state actors very powerful
	multipolarity returning (us, china, eu, india, russia all major players)
	the next few decades likely defined by climate + ai + geopolitical shifts
