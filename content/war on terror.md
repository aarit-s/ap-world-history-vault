[[1900-present]] / [[unit 9]]

context -- post-cold war:

- after fall of soviet union 1991 -- us is the lone superpower
- president george h.w. bush declares "NEW WORLD ORDER"
- 1991 PERSIAN GULF WAR tests this -- saddam hussein invades kuwait
  - us + coalition drive iraq out of kuwait but DON'T oust hussein
  - some in middle east see this as continued us meddling

growing tensions in middle east toward us:

- american pop culture seen as threat to traditional values
- us continued financial + military support of israel
- us military bases in saudi arabia after gulf war
- many in region viewed us as oppressor of muslims (alongside israel)

AL-QAEDA + 9/11:

- al-qaeda = largest, most well-organized terrorist network
- financed + led by OSAMA BIN LADEN
- planned + carried out the SEPTEMBER 11, 2001 attacks on us soil
  - hijacked planes flown into world trade center (nyc), pentagon, fourth crashed in pa
  - ~3000 dead
- separate attacks later on transportation in madrid (2004), london (2005), paris (multiple)
- mumbai attacks 2006, 2008 -- among most deadly

us response -- the WAR ON TERROR:

- 2001 -- us invades AFGHANISTAN, removes taliban from power
  - quick reminder: NO western army had ever won a war in afghanistan (british + soviets failed)
- 2003 -- us invades IRAQ
  - george w. bush claimed saddam hussein was stockpiling WMDs + linked to 9/11
  - NEITHER claim was ever proven right
- 2011 -- us forces locate + kill osama bin laden in pakistan (had been hiding there)
- afghanistan invasion = huge international support
- iraq invasion = no -- damaged us international standing
- costs: ~4500 us soldiers dead, arguably 500,000+ iraqi civilians dead

aftermath:

- rise of ISIS (islamic state) in iraq + syria after us withdrawal
- 2021 -- us finally withdraws from afghanistan, taliban retakes power immediately
- debate over surveillance state, drone strikes, guantanamo bay
- long-term impact on muslim-western relations

big picture:

- first major us military action of 21st century
- blurred lines between war + counterterrorism
- expanded surveillance state (patriot act, nsa)
- gave non-state actors (terrorist networks) huge geopolitical role
- echoes of [[crusades]] framing returned (religious war narrative on both sides)
