[[1200-1450]]
delhi sultanate (1206-1526) -- muslim turkic dynasties ruling over hindu majority north india
	classic case of a small ruling minority over a different-religion majority
	mostly tolerant in practice (had to be, hindus = vast majority)
	centralized bureaucracy with PERSIAN COURT CULTURE
	IQTA land grants (same system as the [[islam]] seljuks) -- military officers given land in exchange for service
	jizya tax on non-muslims
	sufi missionaries spread [[islam]] gradually
	razia sultana -- one of few female muslim rulers in this period
	frequent military campaigns into the deccan (south)
vijayanagara empire (1336-1646) -- hindu empire in south india, resisted muslim expansion
	capital at HAMPI -- massive ruined city you can still visit today
	hindu revival, sophisticated irrigation, navy controlling [[indian ocean trade]] coast
	rajputs (warrior aristocracy) also resisted delhi expansion in central/north india + patronized hindu temple architecture
[[hinduism]] still dominant for most of population
	BHAKTI MOVEMENT -- devotional, challenged caste rigidity
		KABIR -- 15th c. poet-saint, blended hindu + muslim devotion (huge influence on [[sikhism]])
		MIRABAI -- woman poet-saint, devoted to krishna, defied caste + gender norms
		these saints offered a more inclusive spiritual path = mass appeal
[[buddhism]] basically extinct in india by now (overtaken by hinduism)
chola kingdom (850-1250) -- maritime trade in [[indian ocean trade]]
	controlled south india + sri lanka
	trade cities like calicut exported cotton, pepper, spices
	prosperity depended on monsoon wind patterns -- created predictable seasonal trade routes
mahmud of ghazni (11th century) -- launched islamic raids into northern india
	destroyed hindu + buddhist temples
	preview of [[islam]] political dominance in india
jizya tax on non-muslims sparked resistance
some lower-caste hindus converted to [[islam]] -- attracted by message of spiritual equality (similar to early [[christianity]] appeal to lower classes in rome)
cultural [[syncretism]]:
	qutub minar -- islamic architecture with indian influences
	urdu language -- blend of hindi, arabic, persian (still spoken today)

[[1450-1750]]
[[mughal]] takes over from delhi sultanate -- 1526 babur wins battle of panipat
[[mughal]] golden age:
	akbar -- religious tolerance, abolished jizya, married hindu wives
	shah jahan -- built taj mahal
	aurangzeb -- reversed tolerance, persecuted hindus + sikhs, empire starts to fragment
[[sikhism]] founded in punjab
[[europe]]ans show up:
	portuguese (goa)
	british east india company
	french east india company
	dutch
mughal weakens 1700s -> regional powers + european companies fill the vacuum

[[1750-1900]]
british east india company gradually takes over
battle of plassey 1757 -- british beat bengal, start of effective british control
[[sepoy mutiny]] / indian rebellion 1857
british raj -- direct crown rule from 1858
queen victoria "empress of india" 1877
indian national congress founded 1885 -- start of organized nationalism
british cash crops (cotton, indigo, opium) replaced food crops -> famines
deindustrialization -- british flooded india with manufactured cloth, killed indian textile industry
indian indentured laborers shipped globally (caribbean, fiji, africa)
amritsar massacre 1919 -- british troops fired on unarmed crowd in jallianwala bagh, hundreds killed
	radicalized indian opinion against british
	turning point for [[gandhi]] + the independence movement
