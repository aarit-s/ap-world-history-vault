[[1200-1450]]  
ghana (c. 300-1200) -- earliest of the west african empires, controlled trans-saharan gold/salt trade routes

- king tenkamenin most famous ruler
- gradual islamic conversion among elite (population still mostly indigenous animist)
[[mali]] -- west africa, took over from ghana, got rich off [[trans saharan]] gold/salt trade

- [[mansa musa]] -- famous hajj, put mali on the world map
- timbuktu -- center of islamic learning
great zimbabwe (c. 1250-1450) -- southern africa, stone city, controlled gold trade to swahili coast

- massive STONE ENCLOSURE WALLS up to 11 m high -- built WITHOUT MORTAR (advanced masonry)
- economy shifted from cattle pastoralism -> gold mining + export to swahili ports
[[ethiopia]] -- christian kingdom, kept independence, lalibela rock churches  
hausa kingdoms -- west africa, muslim merchant city-states ruled by SARKI (city-state ruler)

- integrated into trans-saharan trade -- famous for fine leather + textiles
swahili coast -- hybrid between bantu and arab traders

- focused on trade with [[indian ocean trade]]
- swahili language = bantu grammar + lots of arabic loanwords
- muslim influence -- these countries converted to [[islam]]
- exported gold, ivory, timber -- imported chinese porcelain, indian cotton, arab glassware
- architecture: coral-stone mosques (e.g., GREAT MOSQUE OF KILWA)

[[1450-1750]]  
[[songhai]] -- took over from mali, askia muhammad, fell to morocco 1591

- timbuktu's SANKORE UNIVERSITY flourishes under songhai patronage -- scholars from across dar al-[[islam]]
asante empire had mad gold ivory and SLAVES  
Kongo kingom -- gold and SLAVES, converted to [[christianity]]  
dahomey -- got powerful from [[atlantic slave trade]]  
problems that came with [[atlantic slave trade]]:
1. gender inequality -- men were more preferred than women as they could do labor
2. rise in men marrying more than one woman (polygyny)
3. cultural synthesis ([[syncretism]])
4. some kingdoms got rich, others destroyed by raiding
5. population stagnated for ~300 years

[[1750-1900]]  
NEGRITUDE MOVEMENT (1930s, see [[interwar period]]):

- french-speaking african + caribbean writers in paris
- celebrated african culture + values, opposed european colonialism
- leopold senghor (poet + later president of senegal), aime cesaire (invented the word), leon damas
- rejected the idea that being "civilized" meant adopting french culture
- inspired future movements for independence + civil rights

sokoto caliphate -- west africa, islamic state founded by usman dan fodio  
zulu kingdom -- shaka zulu unified, fought british at isandlwana (W) and rorke's drift (L)  
muhammad ali in egypt -- modernized, like a north african [[meiji restoration]]  
[[scramble for africa]] -- 1880-1914, carved up at berlin conference 1884-85  
ethiopia + liberia stayed independent

- ethiopia beat italy at adwa 1896
yaa asantewaa -- asante queen mother, led war vs british 1900  
samory toure -- west africa, fought french for 15 years

[[1900-present]] (see [[decolonization]] for full unit 8 detail):

- wwi + wwii served by africans -- raised independence expectations
- negritude movement (1930s) -- cultural pride
- post-wwii: ghana 1957 (kwame nkrumah), kenya 1963 (jomo kenyatta + mau mau), algeria 1962 (FLN), nigeria 1960 (then biafran civil war), angola 1975, south africa apartheid ends 1994 (nelson mandela)
- "organization of african unity" -- nkrumah's pan-africanism legacy
