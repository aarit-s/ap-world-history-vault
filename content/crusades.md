[[1200-1450]] context (crusades themselves are 1095-1291)  
series of religious wars between western [[europe]]an christians and muslims for control of the "holy land" (jerusalem area)

causes:

- pope urban ii wanted to increase his own power + end fighting between european knights
- seljuk turks invaded the holy land + blocked christian pilgrims from entering
- a lot of people saw the opportunity to win wealth or escape troubles at home

effects:

- left a legacy of religious hatred (echoes still happening today)
- power of monarchs in [[europe]] increases (kings consolidated while nobles went off to fight)
- trade increases + expands because of the routes the armies travel while fighting
- BIG cultural transfer back to europe -- access to greek + roman knowledge finally reabsorbed thanks to exposure to the [[islam]]ic world
- medical knowledge, math, philosophy flow back to europe

big picture:

- weakened the byzantine empire (fourth crusade actually sacked constantinople 1204 -- own side!)
- contributed to renaissance + later european expansion
- classic case where the "losers" militarily benefited culturally
