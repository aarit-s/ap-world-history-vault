[[1200-1450]]: people remained in mesoamerica and near the andes  
aztecs (c. 1345-1521):

- huuuuge
- tenochtitlan was massive and expanded massivley
  - built on an island in lake texcoco -- ~200,000 people (one of the biggest cities in the world at the time)
  - causeways + canals + the massive TEMPLO MAYOR pyramid
- created an elaborate system of tribute states
  - tribute = textiles, cacao, and warriors
  - redistributed in elaborate markets like TLATELOLCO
- enslaved people played a large role
incas (early 1400s-1533):

- elbaborate beaurocracy
- very rigid
- mita system: all people needed to work on state projects
  - state farms, mines (potosí silver later), road construction
- admin structure: SAPA INCA (emperor) at top -> CURACAS (provincial governors) -> commoners
- QUIPU -- knotted-string accounting system, allowed centralized record-keeping without writing
mississippian culture (c. 800-1600) -- north america:

- CAHOKIA -- largest pre-columbian city north of mexico (near modern st. louis)
- MONKS MOUND -- ~100 ft tall earthen pyramid, plaza aligned with solar events
- complex religious + political organization, NOT just "scattered tribes"
