[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]  
1968 -- czechoslovakia tries to reform communism, ussr crushes it  
proves the soviet bloc was an empire, not a "voluntary alliance"

setup:

- jan 1968 -- ALEXANDER DUBCEK elected first secretary of czechoslovak communist party
- pushes "SOCIALISM WITH A HUMAN FACE" -- keep communism but allow:
  - freedom of press
  - freedom of speech
  - freedom of travel
  - decentralization of economy
- reform NOT replacement -- still communist, just less authoritarian
- czechs ate it up -- "prague spring" = period of cultural + political opening

soviet response:

- ussr saw this as existential threat -- if czechoslovakia liberalized, every other satellite state might too
- negotiations failed -- dubcek wouldn't roll back reforms
- aug 20-21 1968 -- WARSAW PACT INVASION
- ussr brings in poland, hungary, bulgaria, east germany troops (~500K total) to look like a "collective" action
- tanks roll into prague -- czechs resist nonviolently (sit in the streets, paint over road signs to confuse soviet tanks)
- dubcek arrested + flown to moscow

aftermath:

- "NORMALIZATION" period -- ussr installs hardliner gustav husak, reverses all reforms
- dubcek demoted to forestry official
- jan palach (czech student) sets himself on fire 1969 in protest -> becomes martyr
- "BREZHNEV DOCTRINE" formalized -- ussr will intervene anywhere socialism is "threatened"

big picture:

- exposed the lie that warsaw pact countries were sovereign
- radicalized eastern european dissidents -- they realized reform from within was impossible
- deeply embarrassing for soviet "soft power" -- western communist parties (italy, france) split with moscow over this
- czechoslovakia stayed locked down until VELVET REVOLUTION 1989 (peaceful overthrow of communism)
- dubcek lived to see it -- briefly served as parliament speaker post-1989
- classic AP example of [[cold war]] satellite state dynamics + limits of communist liberalization
