[[1200-1450]] background -- why russia develops differently:
	vladimir the great earlier converted rus to eastern orthodox [[christianity]]
	later tsars rename it russian orthodox church -- tsars claim divine right
	NO renaissance in russia, NO protestant reformation
	[[mongols]] (golden horde) controlled russia 1200s-1400s
		stifled cultural/intellectual growth
		BUT taught moscow: tax collection, military organization, harsh autocratic rule
		russian absolutism is partly modeled after mongol authoritarianism
trade + geography (hidden strength):
	land empire + trade connector
	exports: furs (siberia), leather, iron, tar (shipbuilding), honey, caviar
	arkhangelsk = key port trading with england
	russia not "backward" economically -- just not culturally westernized

ivan iii ("the great") -- foundation of russian autocracy:
	ends mongol dominance
	calls himself TSAR (from "caesar")
	marries niece of last byzantine emperor -> claims to be heir to rome ("third rome")
	seizes novgorod (major trading hub)
	rebuilds the kremlin -> moscow becomes power center
	social structure: tsar > boyars (nobles) > merchants > peasants > serfs
	while western europe REDUCED serfdom, russia STRENGTHENED it (key AP point)

[[1450-1750]]
ivan iv "the terrible" -- first to call himself tsar OFFICIALLY ("tsar of all russia"), expanded into siberia, scary dude
ivan iv expansion + tools of control:
	conquers into kazakhstan + siberia using gunpowder
	reaches pacific -> fur trade expands all the way to alaska
	builds saint basil's cathedral (symbol of victory + power)
	cossacks -- militarized peasant warriors expanding territory, securing volga river
	OPRICHNINA -- secret police / paramilitary in black uniforms, terrorized boyars
		early blueprint for later russian secret police systems
	control of volga -> access to caspian sea -> direct trade with [[safavids]] + [[ottoman]]

time of troubles (1603-1613):
	famine, civil war, foreign invasion, no stable ruler
	ends when ROMANOV dynasty begins (1613-1917)

peter the great (r. 1682-1725):
	goal: modernize russia to compete with europe
	went on a tour of [[europe]] in disguise to learn western tech
	westernized russia HARD -- forced nobles to shave beards, wear european clothes
	eliminated patriarch of russian orthodox church
	nobles forced into state service
	defeats sweden -> access to baltic sea ("warm-water port" problem)
	built st petersburg as a "window to the west" (winter palace = royal residence)
	modernized military, built a navy
	industrial push: shipyards, iron mines (ural mountains)
		BUT forced labor, heavy taxation, increased burden on serfs
	TABLE OF RANKS -- merit-based service system (in theory meritocracy, in practice still autocratic)
	partitions poland (poland disappears as a country until 1919!)
	classic defensive modernization (early version of [[meiji restoration]] / [[tanzimat]])
catherine the great (r. 1762-1796):
	german-born, married into romanovs
	enlightened despot, expanded into poland + crimea
	corresponded with [[enlightenment]] thinkers, condemned torture, supported arts
	BUT strengthened noble privileges + exempted boyars from taxes -- serfdom EXPANDED
	enlightenment rhetoric ≠ real reform
	defeats declining [[ottoman]] in russo-turkish wars -> gains black sea + danube river access
	partitions poland again
	westernizers vs slavophiles debate begins (copy europe vs preserve russian tradition)
	PALE OF SETTLEMENT -- region where jews must live -> increased anti-semitism
		long-term effect: ethnic tension + later jewish migration ([[migration]])
serfdom held russia back -- most of europe abandoned it, russia kept it til 1861

[[1750-1900]]
slow to industrialize -- did it state-driven instead of market-driven
	different from britain/us where workers/capitalists drove it
	state-driven = more discomfort for workers, less middle class
	this is why russia gets marxism really hard later
trans-siberian railway built 1891-1916
serfdom finally abolished 1861 by alexander ii
	but ex-serfs still tied to land + debt
russo-japanese war 1905 -- LOST to [[japan]] (humiliating)
	triggered 1905 revolution
	sets up 1917 russian revolution
expansion east into siberia + central asia + alaska (sold to us 1867)

[[1900-present]] (see [[russian revolution]])
1917 -- tsar nicholas ii abdicates after wwi disasters + rasputin chaos
bolsheviks (lenin) take over -> soviet union
stalin -> 5 year plans, collectivization, gulags
ww2, cold war, eventual collapse 1991
