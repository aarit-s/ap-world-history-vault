[[1750-1900]]  
sense of unity between people based on shared culture, language, history -- loyalty to nation rather than to a king/empire  
product of the [[enlightenment]] + [[revoultions]]

two faces:

- liberatory nationalism -- "we deserve our own country" (latin american [[revoultions]], greek independence, indian national congress)
- aggressive nationalism -- "our nation is superior, we should expand" (germany, japan, [[imperialism]])
- the same idea fuels both (depending on who's wielding it)

key examples:

- french revolution -- nation in arms, citizens not subjects
- latin american revolutions -- creole identity vs spain
- greek independence (1821) from ottomans
- german + italian unification (both 1871) -- many small states -> one nation
- zionism (later 1800s) -- jewish nationalism
- indian national congress (1885)
- young turks (1908)
- pan-africanism + pan-arabism (early 1900s)

how it spread:

- mass print culture (newspapers in vernacular languages)
- universal education in national languages
- conscription armies
- railroads (made "the nation" feel real)

complicates [[imperialism]]:

- european nationalism -> they want colonies for prestige
- colonial nationalism -> resistance to those same empires
- this contradiction explodes in [[1750-1900]] and after
