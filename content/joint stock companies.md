[[1450-1750]]
a limited liability business, often chartered by the state, funded by a group of private investors
key innovation: shareholders can only lose what they invested, NOT their personal wealth
	this means people will actually risk money on long voyages

state-merchant relationship:
	states relied on merchants to succeed (mercantilism needs trade)
	merchants relied on states to give them charters + military backing
	companies got monopoly rights from the crown
	decreased power of state-only sponsored ventures

major examples:
	[[dutch east india company]] (VOC) -- 1602, the goat
	british east india company (EIC) -- 1600, eventually ran [[india]] until [[sepoy mutiny]] 1857
	hudson's bay company -- 1670, fur trade in canada, still exists
	french east india company
	muscovy company -- english trade with [[russia]]
	virginia company -- founded jamestown

big picture:
	first time risk got distributed across many investors
	allowed massive long-term investments (years-long voyages)
	prototype for modern corporations + stock markets
	in [[1750-1900]] this evolves into industrial-era stock markets + multinational corporations
