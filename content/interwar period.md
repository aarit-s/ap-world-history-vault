[[1900-present]] / [[unit 7]]  
period between [[wwi]] and wwii (1918-1939)  
period of transition: failed peace, economic crisis, rise of dictators

THE GREAT DEPRESSION (started 1929):

- cause: us stock market crash october 1929
- germany was paying back us, britain, france monthly -- crash means loans stop, recession
- since all economies were tied together, hit everyone
- massive unemployment, poverty
- people lose faith in governments
- economic crisis -> people demand strong leadership -> democracy WEAKENS
- communists see it as proof capitalism doesn't work

us response: NEW DEAL (FDR + keynesian economics)

- JOHN MAYNARD KEYNES economic theory:
  - advocated for INCREASED gov spending + tax cutting during recession
  - would increase demand + pull economy out
  - government MUST step in to fix it
- new deal "3 Rs":
  - RELIEF for unemployed
  - RECOVERY of economy
  - REFORM of financial system
- created jobs/programs: construction, national parks, etc
- shifted federal government's role toward managing economy + social welfare
- continuity: banks + national parks still exist
- WARNING: if continued indefinitely -> inflation, must be moderated

[[fascism]] rises in italy, germany (see [[fascism]])

- mussolini -> march on rome 1922 -> fascist italy
- hitler -> chancellor of germany 1933 -> nazi germany
[[stalin]] consolidates in soviet union -- 5 year plans, gulags

INDEPENDENCE in [[india]] (gandhi era -- see [[gandhi]]):

- millions of indians served in [[wwi]] for britain expecting greater self-rule
- britain reneged -> indians frustrated
- gandhi emerges using CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE
- salt march, boycott of british cotton
- britain eventually hands over some power

MIDDLE EAST changes (see [[middle east modern]]):

- pan-arabism emerges
- BALFOUR DECLARATION 1917 -- british support jewish home in palestine -> seeds israel-palestine conflict
- [[mustafa kemal]] (ataturk) creates modern secular turkey from ottoman ashes

AFRICA -- promises of self-determination get broken (see [[decolonization]]):

- NEGRITUDE MOVEMENT (1930s) -- african + caribbean writers in paris celebrating black culture
  - leopold senghor, aime cesaire (invented the word), leon damas
  - opposed european colonialism
  - united people of african descent globally
- negative reality:
  - kenya -- soldier settlement scheme rewarded white war veterans with african land
  - south africa -- natives land act 1913 made it illegal for black south africans to own/rent land outside terrible reserves
  - stripped native land + wealth in favor of europeans

EAST ASIA:

- may fourth movement in [[china]] (1919):
  - student-led protest in beijing -> nationwide movement
  - tied to china's frustration losing territory to [[japan]] in [[wwi]]
  - called for resistance to foreign imperialism, political reform
  - laid groundwork for chinese communist party founded 1921
- [[japan]] dug out of great depression FAST:
  - devalued yen, off gold standard, increased gov spending
  - military production became huge factor -> aggressive expansion
  - invaded manchuria 1931, china 1937
- amritsar massacre 1919 in [[india]] -- british fired on civilians (see [[gandhi]])

LATIN AMERICA:

- "estado novo" in brazil under getulio vargas -- intense nationalism + aggressive industrialization
- mexico:
  - "pancho" villa + emiliano zapata -- revolutionary leaders for land reform + peasants rights
  - lazaro cardenas -- stabilized + nationalized oil industry
- US "DOLLAR DIPLOMACY" -- economic over military involvement, "dollars for bullets"
  - gave loans + encouraged private investment in latin america
- US "GOOD NEIGHBOR POLICY" -- shifted to cooperative economic partnership
  - withdrew military from haiti + nicaragua

CULTURAL CHANGES:

- "roaring 20s" in us -- women's social standing changed radically
  - flapper girl lifestyle
  - voted, entered workforce, shorter clothes, smoking, drinking, driving
- albert einstein -- THEORY OF RELATIVITY, e=mc^2, modern physics foundation
- HARLEM RENAISSANCE in nyc:
  - langston hughes, claude mckay, jessie fauset, zora neale hurston
  - racial pride, social critique, african american folklore, jazz/blues rhythms
- art revolutions:
  - CUBISM -- pablo picasso, georges braque -- fragmented subjects into geometric shapes, multiple perspectives at once
  - SURREALISM -- salvador dali, rene magritte -- subconscious, dreamlike paintings
- mexican muralists:
  - diego rivera -- huge bold colors, celebrating mexican history
  - frida kahlo -- personal pain + identity, fantasy + reality

big picture:

- weak peace + weak economy + collapsing democracies = sets up wwii
- [[treaty of versailles]] resentment + great depression + rise of dictators = direct chain to wwii
