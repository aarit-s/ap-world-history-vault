[[1450-1750]]  
maritime empires + transoceanic connections  
this is when the world becomes ONE connected system

why [[europe]] takes off:

- maritime tech borrowed from everywhere:
  - magnetic compass ([[china]])
  - astrolabe (islamic world)
  - lateen sail (arab/[[indian ocean trade]])
  - stern rudder ([[china]])
  - caravel (portuguese -- combined all of it)
- cartographic improvements (mercator projection 1569)
- mercantilism + state competition pushed exploration

key explorers:

- prince henry the navigator -- portuguese, sponsored exploration
- bartolomeu dias -- around cape of good hope 1488
- vasco da gama -- portugal -> india 1498
- columbus -- "discovered" [[americas]] for spain 1492
- magellan -- circumnavigation 1519-22
- zheng he (earlier, [[1200-1450]]) -- ming china BEFORE europe but stopped

empires:

- portugal -- trading post empire in [[indian ocean trade]] (goa, malacca, hormuz)
- spain -- conquered [[americas]] (cortes vs aztec, pizarro vs inca), philippines, silver from potosi
- france -- canada, fur trade with native americans
- england -- north [[americas]], started in [[india]] via east india company
- netherlands -- [[dutch east india company]], indonesia, taiwan briefly, new amsterdam
- [[russia]] -- expanded east into siberia overland (often forgotten)
- [[ottoman]] -- mediterranean naval power, lost lepanto 1571 but stayed strong

global economic system:

- mercantilism -- maximize exports, hoard bullion
- [[joint stock companies]] -- VOC, EIC, hudson bay
- silver from americas -> ming china (china wanted silver, world wanted chinese goods)
- [[columbian exchange]] -- biological revolution
- [[atlantic slave trade]] -- 12 million africans

labor systems in [[americas]]:

- encomienda -- spanish gave colonists right to indigenous labor
- mita (spanish version) -- forced indigenous labor in mines, esp potosi silver
- chattel slavery -- african slaves on plantations
- indentured servitude -- mostly poor europeans, contracted labor
- hacienda system -- big spanish-owned estates

resistance:

- maroons -- escaped slaves (palmares in brazil)
- pueblo revolt 1680 -- pushed spanish out of new mexico for 12 years
- metacom's war (king philip's war) 1675 -- new england natives vs english
- queen nzinga -- ndongo, fought portuguese
- [[japan]] kicked out europeans + christianity entirely

religious + cultural changes:

- [[syncretism]] everywhere (vodun, santeria, virgin of guadalupe)
- protestant reformation in [[europe]] -- 95 theses, luther, calvin
- catholic counter-reformation -- jesuits, council of trent
- [[sikhism]] founded in [[india]]
- mughal religious tolerance under akbar

new social hierarchies:

- casta system in spanish [[americas]] -- elaborate racial ranks
  - peninsulares > criollos > mestizos > mulattoes > indigenous > africans
- creoles resented being below peninsulares -> seeds of latin american [[revoultions]]
