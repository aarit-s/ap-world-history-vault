[[1200-1450]]  
3 religions competed for influence

- [[hinduism]]
- [[buddhism]]
- [[islam]] (spread by muslim merchants via [[indian ocean trade]])
this is similar to [[india]] -- mix of all three

major states:

- funan kingdom (early, before our period) -- early trade hub linking [[india]] + [[china]], spread hindu influence
- khmer empire / angkor (9th-15th c., cambodia) -- building of angkor wat, started hindu and then converted to buddhist
  - advanced irrigation systems -> rice surpluses -> supported massive populations
  - BARAYS -- huge man-made reservoirs that powered the city's water supply (hydraulic city planning)
  - extensive road network linked angkor to outlying provinces
  - built angkor wat AND angkor thom -- structures blend hindu + buddhist art and architecture
  - [[syncretism]] case study -- you can literally see hindu temple converted to buddhist use
- srivijaya (sumatra, earlier) -- buddhist maritime empire that controlled malacca strait
- majapahit (1293-1500, java) -- hindu-buddhist thalassocratic empire (sea-based)
  - tribute system over the malay archipelago
  - produced the NAGARAKRETAGAMA -- royal chronicle = main historical source we have for this empire
  - promoted kshatriya (warrior caste) culture
- sukhothai + later ayutthaya (thailand) -- buddhist
- melaka (malacca) sultanate -- muslim, controlled key choke point in [[indian ocean trade]]

geography matters:

- mainland (vietnam, cambodia, thailand, burma) -- mostly buddhist
- islands (indonesia, malaysia, philippines later) -- mostly converted to islam through merchants
- philippines later became catholic under spain

[[1450-1750]]  
europeans show up

- portuguese take melaka 1511
- [[dutch east india company]] dominates indonesia
- spain takes philippines
