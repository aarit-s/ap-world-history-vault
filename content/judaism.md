oldest [[monotheistic]] religion -- everything else (christianity, islam) builds off it
originated ~2000 BCE in the levant (modern israel/palestine area)

basic teachings:
	one god (yahweh) -- covenant relationship with the jewish people
	holy text: TORAH (first 5 books of hebrew bible) + TALMUD (rabbinic commentary)
	sabbath, kosher dietary laws, circumcision = core practices
	no central authority like the pope -- rabbinic interpretation drives the religion
	"chosen people" doesn't mean superior -- means having a specific covenant with god

key historical beats (compressed):
	abraham, moses, exodus from egypt, david's kingdom, solomon's temple
	2 temples destroyed: 1st by babylonians 586 BCE, 2nd by romans 70 CE
	2nd temple destruction = start of the DIASPORA (jews scattered across the world)
	jews lived as minorities everywhere for the next 2000 years

[[1200-1450]] -- jews in dar al-[[islam]] vs jews in [[europe]]:
	in dar al-islam (esp al-andalus / spain): GOLDEN AGE
		MAIMONIDES (1138-1204) -- huge philosopher, bridged jewish + islamic + greek thought
		jews served as physicians, scholars, translators, merchants
		"dhimmi" status under islam -- protected but second-class, paid jizya tax
	in [[europe]]: way worse
		blamed for [[bubonic plague]] (claimed they poisoned wells)
		excluded from most professions -> pushed into moneylending (which christians weren't allowed to do)
		then resented for being moneylenders -- classic catch-22
		EXPELLED multiple times: ENGLAND 1290, FRANCE 1306, SPAIN 1492 (during reconquista)
	this is why ashkenazi jews ended up concentrated in eastern europe (poland, lithuania, russia)

[[1450-1750]]
spanish expulsion 1492 -- right when columbus is sailing
	sephardic jews scatter to [[ottoman]] empire (welcomed them), netherlands, north africa
	ottoman sultan reportedly mocked spain: "you call ferdinand wise, yet he impoverishes his country to enrich mine?"
"PALE OF SETTLEMENT" in russia (catherine the great, see [[russia]]) -- jews forced to live in restricted region
	feeds long-term anti-semitism + later [[migration]] waves to us

[[1750-1900]]
JEWISH ENLIGHTENMENT (haskalah) -- mirrors broader [[enlightenment]]
	push for jews to integrate into european society as citizens
	napoleon emancipates jews in conquered territories ([[napoleon]])
	BUT also rise of modern racial anti-semitism -- now framed as "race" not religion
	pogroms (anti-jewish riots) in russia 1880s -> mass [[migration]] to us + western europe
ZIONISM emerges late 1800s -- theodor herzl
	movement for a jewish homeland in palestine
	response to european anti-semitism (you can't escape it by assimilating)
	first zionist congress 1897

[[1900-present]]
THE HOLOCAUST -- nazi germany systematically murders 6 MILLION jews ([[wwii]])
	concentration + extermination camps (auschwitz, treblinka, sobibor)
	one of the defining moral catastrophes of human history
	~2/3 of european jewry wiped out
CREATION OF ISRAEL 1948
	post-holocaust + balfour declaration aftermath (see [[middle east modern]])
	britain hands palestine to UN -> partition plan -> israel declares independence
	immediate war with arab neighbors -> palestinian displacement (NAKBA)
arab-israeli wars 1948, 1967 (six-day war), 1973 (yom kippur)
ongoing israeli-palestinian conflict -- still unresolved today
	gaza wars 2008, 2014, 2023-present
modern jewish population:
	~15 million worldwide (still smaller than pre-holocaust ~16.5M)
	largest communities in israel + us
	major denominations: orthodox, conservative, reform, secular

big picture:
	model case study of religious minority living under different host empires
	connects to: [[islam]] dar al-islam, [[europe]] medieval persecution, [[migration]] patterns, [[wwii]] holocaust, [[middle east modern]] israel-palestine
	their suffering across centuries is the dark backdrop to a lot of world history
	survives despite literally everyone trying to wipe them out for 2000+ years
