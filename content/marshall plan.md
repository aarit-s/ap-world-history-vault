[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]
1948 -- us pumps ~$13 BILLION into rebuilding western europe after [[wwii]]
named after secretary of state george marshall

motivation (this is the key AP point):
	on paper: humanitarian aid for war-devastated europe
	actually: STOP THE SPREAD OF COMMUNISM
	post-wwii europe = bombed out, starving, perfect breeding ground for communist parties
	france + italy had strong communist movements that could have won elections
	if western europe went communist, ussr essentially wins the [[cold war]] without firing a shot

what the money did:
	rebuilt factories, infrastructure, agriculture
	stabilized currencies + restarted trade
	required participating countries to coordinate economically (early seed of EU)
	tied western europe ECONOMICALLY to the us -- created the western bloc
	ussr forbade its eastern european satellites from accepting marshall plan aid -> permanent split

results:
	western europe recovered FAST -- "wirtschaftswunder" (german economic miracle)
	communist parties in france + italy lost ground
	directly led to NATO (1949) -- you can't have an economic alliance without a military one
	cemented the iron curtain -- east got nothing comparable, fell behind for decades

big picture:
	one of the most successful foreign aid programs in history
	first time a great power used ECONOMIC AID as a primary geopolitical weapon
	template for later us aid programs (alliance for progress in latin america, etc)
	classic example of [[cold war]] strategy through soft power instead of military
	contrast with the [[treaty of versailles]] disaster -- this time the victors HELPED the losers rebuild
