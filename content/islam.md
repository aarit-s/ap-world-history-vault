origins of islam:

- before muhammad: arabian peninsula dominated by bedouins (hunter-gatherers, small tribes, polytheistic but believed in supreme deity called allah)
- muhammad born 570 CE in mecca (modern saudi arabia), worked as caravan manager
- 595 CE -- khadijah proposes to muhammad, they marry
- 610 CE -- muhammad first hears the call to teach islam through allah's voice (first converts = muslims)
- 622 CE -- muhammad leaves mecca under threat of murder, makes the journey to medina
  - = 1st year of muslim calendar, called the HEGIRA
- 630 CE -- muhammad returns to mecca, defeats his enemies, dedicates the kaaba to allah
- 632 CE -- muhammad dies
basic teachings:

- muhammad is the prophet of allah
- the quran (koran) is the holy book -- covers all messages muhammad received from god
- mecca + medina = two holiest places
- 5 pillars of islam (memorize):
  - shahada -- declaration of faith (no god but god, muhammad is messenger)
  - salah -- ritual prayer 5 times a day
  - zakat -- almsgiving, give portion of wealth to those in need
  - sawm -- fasting during ramadan (9th month of islamic calendar)
  - hajj -- pilgrimage to mecca at least once if able
- jihad -- struggling/striving to be better individually and as community
  - fundamentalist reading: requirement to go to war to preserve faith
- sharia -- islamic law code guiding daily behavior
  - no pork, no alcohol, no enslavement of muslims/christians/jews
  - modern negative connotations from taliban (afghanistan), boko haram (nigeria), sudan

"dar al-islam" = house of islam -- the region where islam is the ORGANIZING principle of society  
co-existing religions inside dar al-islam:

- JUDAISM -- concentrated in iberian al-andalus + parts of the levant; produced scholars like MAIMONIDES
- [[christianity]] -- flourished in crusader states + under byzantine influence
- [[islam]] unified all this through sharia + the UMMAH (community of believers) + arabic as a lingua franca
Was [[monotheistic]]  
abbasid caliphate (750-1258): arab, golden age, baghdad capital

- house of wisdom -- translated greek/persian/indian texts -> kept classical knowledge alive
- preserved EUCLID, PTOLEMY, GALEN, aristotle etc -- this is the stuff europe got back via spain + sicily later
- NASIR AL-DIN AL-TUSI (1201-1274) -- developed trig tables + the "TUSI COUPLE" -> later influenced copernicus
fragmentation of the abbasids (this is the post-10th-century story):

- BUYIDS + SELJUKS take over as autonomous dynasties under nominal abbasid rule
- seljuk turks took over abbasids militarily but kept the caliph as a figurehead
- seljuks introduced IQTA land grants -- conditional military land tenure (similar to fiefs in [[europe]])
- seljuks expand into anatolia -- pushes the byzantine empire back, triggers the [[crusades]]
mamluks -- military slave dynasty in egypt who became a ruling dynasty

- beat the [[mongols]] at ain jalut 1260 -- HUGE deal, this is when mongol expansion finally hit a wall
- also reclaim the levant from the crusaders
[[mongols]] sack baghdad 1258 -- end of abbasid caliphate proper  
established sharia law  
expanded through:

- military -- seljuks into anatolia, mamluks holding the levant + egypt
- muslim merchants ([[indian ocean trade]], [[trans saharan]], [[silk roads]]) -- swahili coast + west africa converted via trade
- muslim missionaries -- sufis especially, adapted to local beliefs (very effective in india + sea)
  - major sufi orders: QADIRIYYA + SHADHILIYYA -- used poetry + music + flexible practices to win converts

sunni vs shia (split was about who succeeds muhammad after he died 632):

- sunni: leader can be any qualified muslim chosen by community (caliphs)
- shia: leader must be a descendant of muhammad through his cousin/son-in-law ali (imams)
- sunni majority empires: [[ottoman]] and [[mughal]]
- shia: [[safavids]] (made shia the state religion of persia)

sufism = mystical branch, big role in spreading islam  
ibn battuta -- moroccan scholar, traveled all of dar al-islam for 30 years  
ibn sina (avicenna) -- philosophy + medicine  
al-khwarizmi -- algebra (literally his book's title)
