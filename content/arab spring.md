[[1900-present]] / [[unit 9]]  
~december 2010 onward  
series of popular (common-people-driven) uprisings across the middle east

biggest factor in spread:

- SOCIAL MEDIA in a region where typically you only heard "state-sponsored" official news
- twitter, facebook, youtube allowed protests to organize + share globally

3 major examples:

- NEDA AGHA-SOLTAN -- shot + killed during the "GREEN WAVE" / GREEN MOVEMENT in IRAN (2009, technically pre-arab spring)
  - her death captured on video, went viral
  - became symbol of iranian dissent
- HOSNI MUBARAK forced to step down as president of EGYPT
  - massive daily protests in CAIRO'S TAHRIR SQUARE
  - ended his ~30 year rule
- MOHAMED BOUAZIZI -- tunisian fruit stand owner
  - set himself on fire to protest continued police brutality, discrimination, government corruption
  - catalyst of the "JASMINE REVOLUTION" in tunisia
  - tunisia's dictator ben ali fled
  - this kicked off the whole arab spring

other countries:

- libya -- gaddafi overthrown + killed (with nato help)
- syria -- assad cracks down -> civil war (still ongoing, displaced millions)
- yemen -- multi-sided civil war
- bahrain -- crushed by saudi intervention
- morocco, jordan -- limited reforms

mixed results:

- tunisia -- only relative success story (transitioned to democracy)
- egypt -- mubarak gone but military took back power under sisi
- libya, syria, yemen -- failed states + civil wars
- overall: hopes for democratization mostly dashed

big picture:

- first major use of social media for revolutionary organizing
- exposed brittleness of authoritarian regimes
- but also showed authoritarianism can adapt
- created the syrian refugee crisis -> european migration debates
- rise of ISIS partially fueled by syrian war chaos
