[[1450-1750]]  
"reformation" comes from REFORM = change  
historically refers to the break from the roman catholic church in western [[europe]]  
weakened church authority + permanently divided [[christianity]]

the protestant reformation -- major reformers:

- martin luther (germany)
  - 95 theses 1517 nailed to church door in wittenberg
  - criticized indulgences (you can pay to get into heaven)
  - argued salvation comes by FAITH ALONE (not works, not church)
  - used the printing press (from [[renaissance]]) to spread his ideas insanely fast
  - translated bible into german -- now common people could read it
- john wycliffe (earlier, england) -- believed bible should be in vernacular languages
- jan hus (bohemia) -- opposed church wealth, executed
- huldrych zwingli (switzerland) -- strict scripture adherence

reformation spreads:

- calvinism (1530s) -- john calvin in geneva
  - predestination -- god has already decided who will be saved
  - those "chosen" should lead church + society
  - french calvinists called HUGUENOTS (faced persecution in france)
  - john knox spreads calvinism in scotland -> presbyterian church

england breaks from rome:

- king henry viii -- wanted divorce, pope refused
- act of supremacy -- makes henry head of the church of england
- church now independent of rome (anglican / church of england)
- queen elizabeth i -- elizabethan settlement, england stays protestant but tolerates catholics for unity

catholic counter-reformation (church fights back):

- council of trent (1545-1563) -- reforms church abuses, reaffirms catholic doctrine, creates index of prohibited books
- jesuits founded by ignatius of loyola -- elite missionary order, education, sent worldwide
- intensified inquisition -- torture used to enforce religious conformity (esp in spain)

global impact:

- reformation weakens catholic power in [[europe]]
- to regain influence, catholic church expands missionary work in asia, [[africa]], [[americas]]
- this religious competition helps drive the age of exploration
- [[syncretism]] in colonies -- catholic + indigenous religions blend (vodun, virgin of guadalupe etc)

political effects:

- wars of religion (30 years war 1618-1648) devastate central europe
- peace of westphalia 1648 -- "cuius regio, eius religio" (the ruler's religion = the state's religion)
- weakened pope's political power -> rise of [[absolutism]] state-by-state
