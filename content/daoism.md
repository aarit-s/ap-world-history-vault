east asian philosophy (NOT a religion in the traditional sense -- doesnt focus on afterlife)  
founded by laozi at end of zhou dynasty (~550 bce)  
contemporary with [[confucianism]]

core idea: BALANCE

- yin and yang symbol = the most common visual
- harmony with nature
- "the dao" = the way / the path of nature

political implications:

- rejected aspects of [[china]] government structure
- rejected the rigid hierarchy of [[confucianism]]
- more individualist + nature-focused

how it interacted with [[buddhism]] in china:

- [[syncretism]] -- mixed to form chan/zen [[buddhism]]
- this is a HUGE example of cultural blending in tang/song [[china]]

cultural impact:

- chinese landscape painting + poetry deeply daoist
- traditional chinese medicine
- martial arts (tai chi etc)
