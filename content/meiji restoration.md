[[1750-1900]]  
1868, [[japan]]  
the GOAT of defensive modernization -- only non-western country to fully industrialize in this period

setup:

- tokugawa shogunate had isolated japan for 250 years
- commodore matthew perry (us navy) showed up in 1853 with steamships and forced japan to open up
- tokugawa looked weak -> samurai overthrew them
- "restored" the meiji emperor (figurehead, but symbolic)

what they did (basically copied europe wholesale):

- abolished feudalism + samurai class
- universal conscription (regular army > samurai)
- western-style constitution (1889) modeled on germany
- modern banking + currency
- state-built railroads, factories, shipyards
- universal education
- sent students + officials abroad to study (iwakura mission)

results -- absolutely cracked:

- beat [[china]] in sino-japanese war 1895
- beat russia in russo-japanese war 1905 (FIRST asian power to beat a european one)
- colonized korea, taiwan, parts of china
- became a world power within 50 years

why it worked when [[self strengthening]] didnt:

- went all-in (institutions + culture, not just tech)
- relatively small + ethnically unified country -- easier to push through
- merchant class already strong from tokugawa era

big picture:

- pairs with [[tanzimat]] (mid) and [[self strengthening]] (L) as the modernization trio
- flips japan from victim of [[imperialism]] to perpetrator of it
