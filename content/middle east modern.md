modern middle east -- after collapse of [[ottoman]] post-[[wwi]]

PAN-ARABISM movement:
	emerged to unite arab people based on shared culture, language, history
	started as rebellion by arab leaders who wanted freedom from [[ottoman]] empire
		ottomans were trying to force their language + culture onto arabs
	after [[wwi]] -- movement got STRONGER due to european [[imperialism]]
		britain + france took control of middle east (sykes-picot agreement, mandate system)
		angered many arabs
		united them to fight off european control + form one independent arab nation
	pan-arabism never fully realized but inspired arab nationalism in many countries

BALFOUR DECLARATION 1917:
	letter from british government
	said britain supported creating a "national home" that wouldn't harm civil/religious rights of jewish people in palestine
	created HUGE conflict because:
		jewish people migrated to palestine
		BUT arab people had lived on that land for a long time + believed it was theirs
	created tension + violence between arab + jewish people
	eventually -> creation of israel 1948 -> ongoing arab-israeli conflict

[[mustafa kemal]] / ATATURK:
	took over as leader after [[ottoman]] empire collapsed
	created modern secular turkey through HUGE reforms:
		separated government from religion (secular state)
		removed islamic law -> replaced with european legal codes
		encouraged western clothing, banned the fez hat
		expanded women's rights -- could vote + run for office
		removed arabic script -> replaced with latin alphabet (easier to read + write)
		closed religious schools -> created free public education system controlled by government
	model of "defensive modernization" similar to [[meiji restoration]]

ARAB-ISRAELI CONFLICT:
	1948 -- creation of state of israel
	immediate war with arab neighbors -- israel wins
	hundreds of thousands of palestinians displaced (the "nakba" / catastrophe)
	1967 six-day war -- israel takes west bank, gaza, golan heights, sinai
	1973 yom kippur war
	1979 camp david accords -- egypt recognizes israel
	ongoing intifadas + peace negotiations
	still unresolved

POST-COLONIAL OIL POLITICS:
	massive oil discoveries in saudi arabia, iraq, iran, gulf states
	OPEC formed 1960 to coordinate prices
	1973 oil embargo (response to us support of israel) -> oil shock + global recession
	wealth + power of oil-producing states grows enormously
	"resource curse" -- wealth doesn't equal democratic governance

IRANIAN REVOLUTION 1979:
	overthrows us-backed shah
	creates SHIA islamic republic under ayatollah khomeini
	cements iran as the shia counterweight to sunni states (returns echoes of [[safavids]] vs [[ottoman]])
	us hostage crisis -> permanent us-iran tension

PERSIAN GULF WAR (1991):
	saddam hussein's iraq invades kuwait
	us + coalition drive iraq out but DON'T oust hussein
	some in middle east see this as continued us meddling

(see [[war on terror]] for 9/11 + iraq/afghanistan + [[arab spring]] for 2010s)

big picture:
	collapse of [[ottoman]] + british/french mandates set up many modern conflicts
	balfour declaration directly creates israel-palestine conflict
	oil makes middle east strategically critical -> us + soviet meddling throughout cold war
	region's borders were drawn by europeans, often ignoring ethnic/religious realities
