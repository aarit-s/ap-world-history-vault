[[1450-1750]] (peak ~1600-1750)
system where monarch has TOTAL political power, not limited by laws, legislature, or nobles
huge AP theme: state-building + legitimacy of power

two main justifications:
	economic strength from age of exploration -- influx of gold/silver = stronger centralized monarchies
	divine right theory -- monarch chosen by god, accountable only to god
key thinkers supporting absolutism:
	james i of england -- wrote the true law of free monarchy (king makes ALL laws, subjects must obey)
	jean bodin (france) -- argued sovereignty must be absolute for order
	thomas hobbes (later) -- humans naturally selfish/violent, life "nasty brutish short" without strong ruler
context: after religious wars of 1500s ([[reformation]]), europeans feared chaos -> strong rulers seemed necessary

SPAIN -- rise + decline of absolute power:
	isabella of castile + ferdinand of aragon united spain (1492 reconquista, expelled jews + muslims)
	philip ii at peak -- defender of catholicism, massive empire in [[americas]]
	conflict with elizabeth i of england (protestant) -> spanish armada defeated 1588 (turning point)
	WHY SPAIN DECLINES:
		weak successors after philip ii
		constant wars = bankrupt treasury
		american gold/silver -> inflation (price revolution)
		expulsion of jews + muslims = lost skilled labor
	BIG IDEA: having gold ≠ having a strong economy (spain relied on bullion instead of building industry)
	cultural peak: cervantes writes don quixote

FRANCE -- the model of absolutism:
	louis xiii + cardinal richelieu set it up
		intendants (royal officials collected taxes, weakened nobles)
		pattern: replace nobles with loyal bureaucrats
	louis xiv "the sun king" -- the GOAT of absolutism
		"l'etat c'est moi" (i am the state)
		stopped calling the estates-general (parliament-equivalent)
		built massive standing army
		palace of versailles -- 10000 workers, kept nobles busy + under surveillance
		minister jean-baptiste colbert -- mercantilism, manufacturing
	war of spanish succession -> peace of utrecht
		no one can rule both france + spain
		balance of power diplomacy begins

PRUSSIA -- militarized absolutism:
	frederick william i -- expanded army massively, military discipline society, mandatory education
	"prussia = army with a state, not state with an army"
	frederick ii (the great) -- enlightened despot
		expanded territory (austria, bohemia, poland)
		absolute power BUT religious tolerance + reforms
	enlightened despot = absolute ruler influenced by [[enlightenment]] ideas

ENGLAND -- THE EXCEPTION:
	does NOT become absolute
	charles i tried to dissolve parliament -> long parliament refused
	english civil war -- cavaliers (king) vs roundheads (parliament)
	oliver cromwell (parliament) wins -> becomes military dictator (lol)
	glorious revolution 1688 -- william iii + mary ii take throne, must sign english bill of rights
	results:
		LIMITED monarchy (constitutional)
		parliament > monarch
		prime minister becomes dominant
		monarch must be anglican
	england = constitutional monarchy
	france = absolute monarchy
	classic LEQ comparison topic

big picture absolutism grows because:
	religious conflict ([[reformation]] aftermath)
	economic growth from exploration
	decline of feudalism
	rise of centralized taxation

big picture absolutism weakens because:
	inflation destabilizes economy (spain)
	expensive wars drain treasury
	[[enlightenment]] ideas challenge divine right -> [[revoultions]]

memory anchors:
	spain = gold but no industry -> inflation
	france = versailles + sun king
	prussia = military machine
	england = parliament wins
