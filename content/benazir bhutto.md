1953-2007 -- pakistani politician, first woman elected to lead a muslim-majority country  
prime minister of pakistan twice (1988-90, 1993-96)  
assassinated 2007

background:

- daughter of zulfikar ali bhutto (former prime minister, executed by military 1979)
- educated at harvard + oxford -- english-speaking elite
- took over pakistan people's party (PPP) after father's execution
- classic case of dynastic political succession in south asia (parallel to nehru-gandhi family in [[india]], aquino in philippines)

what she stood for:

- women's rights + education -- huge symbol globally
- democracy vs military rule (pakistan kept flipping between elected govs + military coups)
- modernization + economic liberalization
- tense relationship with islamist parties + the powerful pakistani military

issues during tenure:

- corruption allegations against her + her husband asif ali zardari ("mr. 10 percent")
- dismissed both times by the president
- exiled to london + dubai during musharraf military era

assassination:

- returns to pakistan 2007 to run for office again
- dec 27 2007 -- shot + bombed at a campaign rally in rawalpindi
- taliban-linked group claimed responsibility
- husband zardari became president 2008-13

big picture:

- rise of women leaders in late 20th c. -- indira gandhi ([[india]]), margaret thatcher (uk), golda meir (israel), corazon aquino (philippines), angela merkel (germany later)
- BUT bhutto specifically broke the barrier in the muslim world -- huge symbolic weight
- women leaders often disprove gender stereotypes about leadership in crisis
- her assassination shows the violent backlash against women's leadership + secular politics in some regions
- ongoing tension between democracy + military rule in pakistan continues today
- connects to broader [[unit 9]] themes: women's rights as global movement, dynastic politics in postcolonial states, terrorism + the [[war on terror]] era
