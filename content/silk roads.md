[[1200-1450]]  
overland trade network across eurasia, china <-> mediterranean  
luxury goods from [[china]]: silk (duh), porcelain, tea  
growing demand -> growing production -> commercialization in song [[china]]

innovations:

- caravanserai -- inns about a day apart from each other for protection + safety
- camels (bactrian for steppe, dromedary for desert) -- replaced wagons
- [[money econmies]] -- paper money + credit, "flying cash" started in china
- improved saddles
- gunpowder (innovation that traveled, not just used here)

cultural + tech transfer:

- [[buddhism]] spread from india to china + japan via silk roads earlier
- [[islam]] spread along these routes
- gunpowder, printing, paper money: china -> west
- math + astronomy: islamic world -> everyone

[[mongols]] = peak silk roads (pax mongolica)

- one empire = safe travel
- marco polo + ibn battuta + rabban bar sauma all traveled this network
- BUT also spread the [[bubonic plague]]

decline:

- end of mongol empire fragmented the routes
- [[indian ocean trade]] became more important (cheaper to ship by sea)
- [[europe]]ans eventually went around by sea entirely

rise of trading cities along the route -- samarkand, bukhara, kashgar
