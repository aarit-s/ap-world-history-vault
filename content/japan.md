[[1200-1450]]
japan started off following [[china]] (borrowed [[buddhism]], writing system, govt structure)
practiced SELECTIVE BORROWING -- adopted then modified everything to fit japanese culture
	classic continuity AND change example for AP essays
indigenous religion: shinto -- nature-based belief system, coexisted with imported buddhism
but went its own way -- developed feudal system similar to [[europe]]
minamoto clan defeated fujiwara, established shogunate system
shoguns (military leaders) had real power, emperor was figurehead
feudal hierarchy:
	shogun -> daimyo (regional lords/landowners) -> samurai (warrior class) -> peasants
samurai = warrior class with their own honor code (bushido) -- honor, loyalty, death before surrender
AP COMPARISON ALERT: japanese feudalism parallels european feudalism
	key differences: samurai used LIGHTER armor than knights
	bushido replaced chivalry as the warrior code
heian period -> kamakura -> ashikaga shogunates

[[1450-1750]]
tokugawa ieyasu won battle of sekigahara 1600 -> tokugawa shogunate
interested in trading for gunpowder so he likes to go trade with [[europe]]
people are converting to [[christianity]] and now hes like hell nah ian letting that happen
1635 -- "sakoku" isolation policy:
	kicked out europeans
	banned christianity, executed converts
	only allowed limited dutch trade at one port (dejima, nagasaki)
	japanese forbidden to leave
internal stability + economic growth during isolation
edo (tokyo) became one of the largest cities in the world
cultural achievements during tokugawa:
	haiku poetry (matsuo basho)
	kabuki theater
	distinctive pagoda architecture

[[1750-1900]]
commodore matthew perry (us navy) showed up 1853 with steamships
forced japan to open up -- "unequal treaties" like [[china]]
tokugawa looked weak -> samurai overthrew them
[[meiji restoration]] 1868 -- went FULL western, industrialized fast
	symbolic of the economic overhaul: TOYODA LOOM WORKS (later becomes toyota)
beat [[china]] in sino-japanese war 1895
beat [[russia]] in russo-japanese war 1905 (first asian power to beat european power)
colonized korea (1910), taiwan, parts of china
went from victim of [[imperialism]] to perpetrator -- only non-western country to do this

[[1900-present]]
during great depression: japan dug itself out FAST
	devalued the yen (fights inflation, encourages spending)
	took money off the gold standard
	increased government spending + ran deficits + expanded credit
	worked closely with big companies
	military production became huge factor -> aggressive expansion
	this set up [[wwii]] in the pacific
expanded into manchuria 1931, china 1937 (rape of nanjing)
attacks pearl harbor dec 7 1941 -> us enters wwii
defeated 1945 (atomic bombs hiroshima + nagasaki)
post-war: us occupation, democratized, economic miracle 1960s-80s
