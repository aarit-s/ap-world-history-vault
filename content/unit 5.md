[[1750-1900]]  
revolutions -- intellectual, political, industrial

intellectual cause:

- [[enlightenment]] -- reason, natural rights, social contract
- scientific revolution before that (newton, galileo)

political [[revoultions]]:

- american (1776) -- first big enlightenment-driven revolution, became the template
- french (1789-99) -- overthrew the king, declaration of rights of man
- haitian (1791-1804) -- toussaint louverture, only successful slave revolt -> independent country
- latin american:
  - simon bolivar -- liberated venezuela, colombia, ecuador, peru, bolivia
  - jose de san martin -- argentina, chile, peru
  - miguel hidalgo -- mexico
  - creoles led most of these against spanish peninsulares
- unification:
  - italy (1871) -- garibaldi, cavour, victor emmanuel
  - germany (1871) -- bismarck, blood and iron, franco-prussian war

ideologies (memorize the 4):

- liberalism -- free markets, individual rights, constitutional govt (adam smith, john stuart mill)
- nationalism -- loyalty to nation-state (drove unification + later anti-colonial movements)
- socialism -- workers should control production
- marxism / communism -- karl marx + friedrich engels, communist manifesto 1848
  - bourgeoisie (factory owners) vs proletariat (workers)
  - predicts proletariat revolution

economic revolution:

- [[industrial revolution]] -- starts britain, spreads
- mercantilism dies, free trade wins (adam smith, wealth of nations 1776)
- [[joint stock companies]] 2.0 -> stock markets

social reforms:

- abolition of slavery
  - britain ended slave trade 1807, slavery 1833
  - us 1865, brazil 1888
- abolition of serfdom in [[russia]] 1861 (alexander ii)
- women's rights:
  - mary wollstonecraft -- vindication of the rights of woman 1792
  - seneca falls convention 1848 -- declaration of sentiments
  - olympe de gouges -- french, declaration of the rights of woman
  - women's suffrage movements grow
- labor unions form to push back on factory conditions

sets up [[unit 6]] -- once industrialized, [[europe]] needs raw materials + markets -> [[imperialism]]
