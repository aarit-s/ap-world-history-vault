[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]  
process by which european colonies gained independence  
mostly post-wwii (1945-1975) but starts earlier with [[gandhi]] / [[india]]

why decolonization happened after wwii:

- european powers exhausted from wwii (broke + bombed out)
- [[wwi]] + wwii used colonial troops -- created expectations + organizational experience
- soviet union + us both anti-colonial (different reasons but same outcome)
- UN charter committed to self-determination
- colonized peoples saw europeans defeat each other -> myth of european superiority broken
- nationalist movements organized for decades

[[india]] sets the template:

- [[gandhi]] -- civil disobedience movement
- 1947 independence + partition into india + pakistan

INDEPENDENCE in [[africa]]:

- finally after wwii, the african colonies start to push for independence
- general POSITIVES:
  - trend of increasing democracy in african countries
  - large collective of independence leaders -- jomo kenyatta (kenya), kwame nkrumah (ghana), nelson mandela (south africa)
- general NEGATIVES:
  - some independence leaders themselves became corrupt dictators using military rule over democracy
  - civil wars + ethnic conflicts continued to break out (often along borders drawn at [[scramble for africa]])

GHANA -- 1st african country to gain independence

- first president: KWAME NKRUMAH
- huge emphasis on PUBLIC WORKS (esp hydroelectric plants)
- set up a one-party state by 1964 -- claims dictatorship
- soon accused of corruption + bad spending
- HUGE supporter of "PAN-AFRICANISM" -- created idea of "ORGANIZATION OF AFRICAN UNITY" (african version of european union)

ALGERIA

- french colony for a long time -- lots of french settlers (like south africa, white minority power)
- violent war breaks out 1954 -- colonial gov vs NATIONAL LIBERATION FRONT (FLN)
- 1958 -- president CHARLES DE GAULLE asks french if they want independent algeria
- huge immigration problem -- french-algerians rush back to france
- de gaulle's vote allows fln to take over algerian gov -> SOCIALIST AUTHORITARIAN government
- 1991 -- ALGERIAN CIVIL WAR breaks out as factions try to replace one-party system
- on/off military state of emergency until ARAB SPRING

KENYA

- east coast of [[africa]], old "zanj cities" region, british colony
- lots of asians in kenya from building of mombasa-kisumu railway
- independence movement: native group called MAU MAU did terrorist activities starting 1952 against british colonial government
- terror campaigns work -> kenya gains independence 1963
- new leader: JOMO KENYATTA -- sets up one-party state called KANU (kenyan african national union)
- after kenyatta's death -- daniel moi takes power -> years of bad decisions, corruption, violence
- displaced ~600,000 kenyans

ANGOLA

- 1975 -- gains independence from portugal
- ushers in 27-year civil war
- mostly about country's massive diamond mines
- but ALSO becomes a cold war proxy:
  - soviet union backs MBUNDU TRIBE (most of MPLA -- popular movement for liberation of angola)
  - us backs BAKONGO TRIBE (most of FLNA -- national front for liberation of angola)
- communism wins eventually

NIGERIA

- gains independence 1960 from britain
- most populous state in [[africa]]
- most oil deposits (along niger river delta)
- BIAFRAN CIVIL WAR breaks out 1967
  - igbos (pronounced ee-bose) -- westernized christian tribe from southeast wanted to secede + make own homeland
  - igbos lose but religious + ethnic tension remains
  - creation of 36 states within nigeria to break down ethnic barriers

SOUTH AFRICA

- complicated history -- 15% of population (white) controlled ALL gov power
- 1948 -- gets even more complicated with APARTHEID
  - strict policy of racial segregation
  - "PASS LAWS" said where black south africans could move freely
- 1964 -- nelson mandela (leader of ANC -- african national congress) jailed for life for "agitating" against apartheid
- 1980s -- south africa is a pariah state internationally
- new president F.W. DE KLERK gets mandela released from prison
- 1994 -- 1st free elections ever -- mandela elected president

bigger pattern -- powerful nations exploit land + people for economic gain:

- treaty of waitangi (1840) -- british deceived maori, seized land + rights in new zealand
- indian removal act (1830) -- us forced native americans west
- fort laramie treaty (1868) -- us repeatedly broke treaties with native americans
- berlin conference (1885) -- european powers divide africa with no african participation ([[scramble for africa]])
- suez crisis (1956) -- egypt nationalizes suez canal -> conflict over strategic trade route (similar to malacca or singapore)

big picture:

- colonial borders + ethnic divisions left behind by europeans cause civil wars for decades
- cold war turned many decolonization conflicts into proxy wars
- "neocolonialism" -- former colonizers still control colonies economically through corporations + loans
