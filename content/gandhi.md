[[1900-present]] / [[unit 7]] / [[unit 8]]
mohandas gandhi (called "mahatma" = great soul) -- leader of indian independence movement
emerges from a lawyer career in south africa fighting for indian rights there

context for [[india]] independence:
	millions of indians served in [[wwi]] for britain based on promise of greater self-rule
	after war, indians frustrated by limited british reforms
	AMRITSAR MASSACRE april 1919:
		thousands of indians gathered in jallianwala bagh public garden in amritsar to protest + attend a religious festival
		british troops surrounded them, opened fire for 10 minutes
		hundreds killed, panic spread
		became HUGE turning point in independence movement

gandhi's strategy -- CIVIL DISOBEDIENCE:
	must be NONVIOLENT to be effective
	believed in love + truth + respect toward people but not the unjust law
	encouraged indians to:
		boycott british goods (especially cotton)
		boycott british schools
		resign from government titles + offices
		spin their own cloth (the spinning wheel = the symbol)
	had broad appeal -- reached out to ALL indians (rich, poor, hindu, muslim, lower caste)

big actions:
	SALT MARCH 1930 -- led indians to make salt from sea water in defiance of british salt monopoly
		one of the most iconic protests in history
		inspired others to join
	hunger strikes
	jail time as moral capital

international impact:
	nonviolent tactics gained international attention
	many countries became sympathetic to indian cause
	inspired later movements (martin luther king jr in us civil rights, mandela)

eventually britain hands over some power to india to govern itself

partition + tension:
	GANDHI vs JAWAHARLAL NEHRU vs MUHAMMAD ALI JINNAH
	tensions hit peak during cabinet mission of 1946
		plan proposed: keep india united but divided into clusters of provinces
		gandhi + jinnah initially showed interest
		nehru then publicly stated central government would override provinces
		jinnah withdraws support
		jinnah calls for "DIRECT ACTION DAY" -> triggered widespread communal violence
	1947 -- india gains independence but PARTITIONED into:
		india (hindu majority) led by nehru
		pakistan (muslim majority) led by jinnah
	one of largest forced migrations in history -- ~15 million displaced
	hindu-muslim violence killed up to 2 million
	gandhi assassinated 1948 by hindu nationalist who thought he was too soft on muslims

big picture:
	first major successful anti-colonial movement (sets template for [[decolonization]] in africa)
	nonviolence as a viable political strategy
	but partition shows even successful independence can have huge human cost
