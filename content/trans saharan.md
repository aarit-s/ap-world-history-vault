[[1200-1450]]  
trade route across the sahara desert connecting north [[africa]] (muslim, mediterranean) with west africa (sub-saharan)  
expanded a lot in this period

key innovation: camel saddle

- north african + arab style saddles let camels carry way more
- camels = "ships of the desert" -- could go a week without water

what was traded:

- going south -> north: GOLD (huge for europe + middle east), salt, slaves, ivory
- going north -> south: cloth, books, horses, manufactured goods

major empires that controlled it:

- ghana (earlier)
- [[mali]] -- got rich off gold + salt trade
- [[mansa musa]] -- famous hajj 1324, put mali on world maps, gave away so much gold he crashed cairo's economy
- [[songhai]] (later, [[1450-1750]]) -- askia muhammad

cultural effects:

- [[islam]] spread south into west africa (rulers + merchants converted, peasants kept indigenous beliefs longer)
- timbuktu became major islamic learning center -- universities, libraries, scholars
- swahili-style hybrid culture

later: gunpowder lead to the [[gunpowder empires]]
