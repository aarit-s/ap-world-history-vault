[[1200-1450]]  
huuuuuuge  
nomadic steppe people from mongolia  
genghis khan unified the mongol tribes ~1206 and started conquering everyone  
established largest contiguous land empire EVER (eurasia coast to coast)  
why they were so dominant:

- mounted archers -- horse + composite bow combo unmatched
- siege weapons learned from chinese
- terror tactics -- if a city resisted, they killed everyone (psychological warfare)
- mobility + organization beyond anything sedentary states had
- decimal organization -- units of 10, 100, 1000 for military efficiency
- practiced religious tolerance + protected trade routes
- adopted technologies from conquered peoples:
  - gunpowder from [[china]]
  - persian bureaucracy
  - chinese engineers

separated into 4 khanates after genghis died:

- yuan dynasty -- [[china]], kublai khan, marco polo visited
- chagatai khanate -- central asia
- il-khanate -- persia / middle east
- golden horde -- [[russia]] / eastern europe

PAX MONGOLICA (mongol peace):

- one empire = safe travel across all of eurasia
- merchants, missionaries, diplomats moved freely
- HUGE boom in [[silk roads]] trade
- tech + cultural transfers in both directions:
  - gunpowder + printing + paper money: china -> west
  - islamic astronomy + medicine -> china
- marco polo + ibn battuta both traveled this network
- BUT: also spread the [[bubonic plague]] along the same routes (~1347)
- ended the abbasid caliphate (1258 -- sack of baghdad, see [[islam]])
- ended the song dynasty (1279) -> yuan replaces it

decline:

- individual khanates declined separately
- yuan kicked out by ming 1368
- mamluks beat them at ain jalut 1260 (rare L)
- timur (tamerlane) -- last big mongol-ish conqueror, 1370s-1405

legacy:

- connected world more than ever before
- end of mongol = end of the most integrated old-world system until [[europe]]ans showed up
