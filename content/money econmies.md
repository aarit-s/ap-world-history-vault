how the world moved from barter -> coins -> paper -> credit -> digital
huge AP theme because economic systems shape everything else (state power, social class, trade networks)

origins:
	barter = direct trade of goods (problem: needs "double coincidence of wants")
	commodity money -- shells, salt, cattle (still has practical use)
	metallic coins -- standardized, durable, divisible (lydia ~600 BCE invented stamped coins)
	silver + gold dominated long-distance trade for centuries

[[1200-1450]]
PAPER MONEY originates in [[china]]
	"FLYING CASH" started in tang dynasty -- merchants got paper receipts they could exchange for coins elsewhere
	song dynasty = first govt-issued paper currency (the "jiaozi")
	[[mongols]] adopted + spread the idea across their empire
	marco polo brought the concept back to europe (europeans were like "wait, paper = money?? insane")

CREDIT systems also from china:
	letters of credit -- merchants didn't have to carry actual coins through bandit territory
	parallel: islamic merchants used SAKK (origin of the word "check")
	[[silk roads]] + [[indian ocean trade]] depended on these credit instruments

medieval [[europe]] catches up slow:
	usury (charging interest) banned by catholic church -- huge brake on banking
	loophole: jewish moneylenders allowed to charge interest (since they weren't bound by catholic rules) -- see [[judaism]]
	italian city-states (florence, venice, genoa) develop early banking despite the rules
	MEDICI BANK (florence, 1397+) -- pioneers double-entry bookkeeping, branch networks across europe
	bills of exchange let merchants move "money" between cities without physical transport

[[1450-1750]]
SILVER + GOLD FLOOD from americas changes everything ([[columbian exchange]])
	spanish silver from POTOSÍ + zacatecas -> spain -> rest of europe -> china
	china DEMANDED silver as tax payment (single-whip reform under ming) -> sucked in global silver
	created the first truly GLOBAL economy -- silver from peru paying for silk from china
	caused PRICE REVOLUTION in europe -- massive inflation, peasants suffered
RISE OF JOINT STOCK COMPANIES (see [[joint stock companies]] + [[dutch east india company]]):
	investors pool money + share risk + share profits
	allowed financing of huge expensive voyages
	BANK OF AMSTERDAM 1609 -- first major central bank
	BANK OF ENGLAND 1694 -- still around today

[[1750-1900]]
[[industrial revolution]] requires more capital than ever
	stock exchanges expand (london, new york)
	commercial banks fund factories + railroads
	GOLD STANDARD adopted by major economies -- currencies pegged to gold
	this stabilized international trade but made monetary policy super rigid

[[1900-present]]
gold standard collapses in great depression (see [[interwar period]])
	BRETTON WOODS 1944 -- new system, dollar pegged to gold, other currencies pegged to dollar
	1971 -- nixon ends dollar-gold convertibility -> fiat money era begins
	all modern money is now backed by government promise, not commodity
DIGITAL MONEY explosion:
	credit cards 1950s+ -- consumer credit on demand
	online banking 1990s+
	bitcoin 2009 -- first cryptocurrency (bypasses governments)
	mobile payments -- m-pesa in kenya 2007 was huge for unbanked africans

big picture:
	more abstract money = more economic complexity possible
	whoever controls money supply controls a LOT of political power
	the song dynasty paper money -> european banking -> joint stock -> central banking -> fiat -> crypto pipeline is a whole AP DBQ topic
	classic AP theme: economic systems and how they integrate (or fragment) the world
