[[1750-1900]] / [[unit 6]]
happens right after the [[industrial revolution]]
nationalism is important which lead to lots of bitter rivalry between european powers
race is hugggge means of discrimination (scientific racism) in [[europe]] against [[africa]] + others

ideologies of empire:
	social darwinism -- "survival of the fittest" applied to nations + races
		western industrial societies are "best suited" for current global environment
		used to justify conquest as natural
	scientific racism -- pseudoscience ranking races (skull measurements etc), all bs
	white man's burden (kipling poem) -- "duty" to civilize
	mission civilisatrice (france) -- civilizing mission
	"god, gold, glory" (older motivation, still applies)

methods:
	state takeovers (belgium leopold ii personally owned congo, atrocities on genocide scale)
	diplomacy + warfare
	drawing dumb borders in africa ([[scramble for africa]] / berlin conference)
	settler colonies like how britain did in [[americas]] / australia / south africa
	americans conquered neighbors (mexico, native americans, philippines, hawaii)
	economic imperialism -- dont take over the country, just control its economy ([[opium wars]])
	spheres of influence (esp [[china]])

tech that made it possible:
	steamships (go upriver into africa)
	quinine (treat malaria, makes africa survivable for europeans) -- THIS is the unlock for [[scramble for africa]]
	maxim gun (early machine gun)
	telegraph + railroads (control colonies fast)

key figures of imperialism:
	cecil rhodes -- mining magnate + politician, "cape to cairo" vision in africa, founder of de beers diamonds + rhodes scholarship
	david livingstone -- missionary who fought against the slave trade in africa
	king leopold ii of belgium -- personally owned congo, atrocity scale = genocide
	james cook -- "founded" new zealand for britain (encountered maori)
	otto von bismarck -- germany's chancellor
		"blood and iron" -- military-industrial unification
		REALPOLITIK -- pragmatism in politics, do what works, drop idealism
		hosted berlin conference 1884-85 -> [[scramble for africa]]

specific imperial events to know:
	white australia policy (1901) -- racist immigration restriction act, kept australia european
	open door policy (us, 1899-1900) -- demanded all powers have equal trade rights in [[china]]
		actually helped strengthen us influence
	roosevelt corollary -- extension of monroe doctrine
		latin america = us "domain", europe stay out of western hemisphere
	lord macartney -- led western influence into [[china]] (failed first mission 1793)

trans-siberian railway -- russia's symbolic IMPERIAL infrastructure (1891-1916)
	but unlike germany's success, russia's modernization mostly fails -> 1917 [[russian revolution]]

major colonial holdings:
	britain -- india, egypt, south africa, nigeria, kenya, australia, canada, etc
	france -- west + north africa, indochina (vietnam, laos, cambodia)
	germany (latecomer) -- tanganyika, namibia, cameroon
	belgium -- congo
	netherlands -- indonesia
	[[japan]] -- korea, taiwan, parts of china (asian imperialism)
	us -- philippines, puerto rico, hawaii after 1898

resistance ([[unit 6]] obsesses over this):
	asking questions about authority
	sense of nationalism (now anti-colonial)
	rebellion: [[sepoy mutiny]], [[boxer rebellion]], xhosa cattle killing, ghost dance
	modernization: [[tanzimat]], [[self strengthening]], [[meiji restoration]]
	new states: sokoto caliphate, zulu kingdom

economic effects:
	export economies exist -- colonized people stop being just for subsistence
	monoculture (one cash crop) made colonies fragile
	wealth flowed to europe, infrastructure built only to extract resources
	suez canal (1869), panama canal (1914) -- imperial infrastructure
