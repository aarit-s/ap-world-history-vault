[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]  
"MAD" -- the doctrine that if either us or ussr launched nukes, the other would launch back, and both sides + most of humanity would be wiped out

how it worked:

- both sides built up massive nuclear arsenals -- ICBMs, hydrogen bombs, nuclear submarines
- "second-strike capability" -- even if you got nuked first, you had enough to retaliate
- this guaranteed MUTUAL destruction -> made attacking SUICIDAL
- defined the global atmosphere of FEAR from the 1950s-1991

consequences:

- no direct war between us + ussr the entire [[cold war]] (despite massive tension)
- all conflicts pushed into PROXY WARS in third countries (korea, vietnam, afghanistan, angola, nicaragua)
- massive arms race -- by 1980s both sides had ~30,000 nukes each (overkill many times over)
- created entire industries around early-warning systems, missile defense, espionage

near-misses:

- [[cuban missile crisis]] 1962 -- closest the world ever came to MAD activating
- 1983 stanislav petrov incident -- soviet officer correctly identifies false alarm + saves the world
- 1995 norwegian rocket incident -- yeltsin almost launched

cultural impact:

- huge anxiety in pop culture -- "duck and cover" drills, fallout shelters, dr. strangelove (1964 film)
- "acceptable losses" thinking became a real military concept
- gen x grew up assuming nuclear war was likely

end of MAD logic (kinda):

- end of [[cold war]] 1991 reduced the immediate threat
- START treaties cut arsenals significantly
- BUT nukes still exist -- now also held by china, india, pakistan, north korea, israel, uk, france
- doctrine still applies between modern nuclear powers

big picture:

- the most insane safety mechanism in human history -- "we won't kill each other because we'd both die"
- ironically credited with KEEPING THE PEACE between superpowers for 45 years
- demonstrates the [[cold war]] as a PSYCHOLOGICAL conflict, not just military
- humanity now permanently capable of self-extinction (started after [[wwii]])
