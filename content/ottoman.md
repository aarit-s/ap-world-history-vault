turkic origin -- traces back to timur "the lame" / tamerlane

- timur ended [[mongols]] dominance in central asia
- "godfather" of turkic empires
- follows the ghazi ideal -- ideal islamic warrior spreading [[islam]]
- monumental architecture showed power + wealth
- his empire collapsed quickly but proved gunpowder + artillery were the future

mehmed ii ("the conqueror"):

- 1453 -- captures constantinople -> renames it ISTANBUL, ends byzantine empire
- constantinople used to be last beating heart of roman empire and once they won that they could really do whatever they wanted
- largest + longest-lasting [[gunpowder empires]]
- strengthens control of bosporus strait
- builds topkapi palace (royal residence)
- expands into black sea + southeastern europe
- invests heavily in ottoman NAVY (only gunpowder empire to actually build a real navy)
- leads to attacks in italy + north africa

devshirme: they took christian boys (ages 8-20) from conquered lands, converted them into islam and trained them for government or military service

- janissaries -- elite military unit formed through devshirme
  - loyal to the sultan, NOT to local leaders
  - serve as soldiers, bureaucrats, diplomats
- clever insurance: people won't revolt because they'd have to fight their own kids

suleiman i ("the lawgiver" / "the magnificent") -- peak of ottoman power:

- major victories: rhodes (1522), hungary (1526), nearly captures vienna (1529)
- builds forts, mosques, infrastructure (suleimani mosque in istanbul)
- creates a modern legal system
- wore huge turban ("onion") to display power

[[1450-1750]]  
opened up their land for the jews and tolerated them  
ottoman economy:

- expansion required constant revenue (huge area = huge military = huge tax burden = cave-in)
- tribute from conquered lands
- heavy taxation of peasants
- religious tolerance for christians + jews -- as long as they paid taxes (jizya)
- informal coffeehouse trade negotiations + formal trade agreements with france
- key goods: cotton, tobacco, coffee
ottoman social structure:

- sultan at top
- rising tension with military aristocracy (janissaries)
- viziers serve as advisors
- HAREM POLITICS -- wives + concubines influenced succession (sultanate of women era)
- middle class = merchants + artisans
- peasants + slaves at bottom (many captured by barbary pirates, used in navy)

decline:

- power struggles between sultans + janissaries
- weak rulers after suleiman
- increased influence of women + eunuchs via harem politics
- over-taxation of peasants
- battle of lepanto (1571) -- major naval defeat, lost mediterranean dominance
- gradual retreat toward istanbul
