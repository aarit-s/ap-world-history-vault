[[1750-1900]]
under english/british control for centuries -- core grievance running through irish history

irish potato famine (1845-1849):
	potato blight wiped out the staple crop
	~1 million died of starvation + disease
	~1 million emigrated -- mostly to us, britain, australia
	british response widely seen as criminally negligent (continued exporting other irish food during the famine)
	huge engine of [[migration]] -- defines irish-american identity

[[1900-present]]
search for national identity + independence
	political change attempted first -> didnt work
	violent action: MICHAEL COLLINS + the EASTER UPRISING of 1916
	creation of IRISH FREE STATE (most of ireland) 1922
	but NORTHERN IRELAND stays as part of england
	-> creation of IRISH REPUBLICAN ARMY (IRA)
	"the troubles" 1968-1998 -- catholic vs protestant violence in northern ireland
	good friday agreement 1998 mostly ended the conflict
	republic of ireland today = independent + EU member
	northern ireland still uk

big picture:
	classic case of national identity formation under colonial rule (parallel to [[india]], italy, germany, vietnam, etc)
	irish diaspora is one of the most influential in world history (kennedys, etc)
