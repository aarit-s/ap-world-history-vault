[[1750-1900]]  
the OTHER half of [[unit 6]] -- people moved more in this period than ever before in history  
[[industrial revolution]] caused both the push (no jobs at home) and the pull (jobs in factories elsewhere)

push factors:

- famine -- irish potato famine (1845-49) sent ~1m irish to us
- persecution -- jewish pogroms in russia
- overpopulation in rural areas
- end of slavery -> demand for cheap labor (created indentured servitude)

pull factors:

- industrial jobs in cities (manchester, chicago, sao paulo)
- cheap/free land (us homestead act, australia)
- gold rushes (california 1849, australia 1851, south africa 1886)
- railroads needing labor

major flows:

- europeans -> [[americas]] (~50 million between 1815-1914)
  - irish, italian, german, jewish, polish
- chinese -> southeast asia, [[americas]], caribbean, africa
  - coolie trade -- contracted labor, basically slavery 2.0
  - built us transcontinental railroad
- indians -> caribbean, fiji, mauritius, south africa, east africa
  - gandhi started in south africa
- japanese -> hawaii, brazil, california

new social patterns:

- gender imbalance -- usually men migrated first
- ethnic enclaves -- chinatowns, little italys, jewish lower east side
- remittances -- money sent home (huge for sending countries)

backlash (nativism):

- chinese exclusion act (us 1882) -- first race-based us immigration ban
- white australia policy (1901)
- anti-irish, anti-catholic in us
- pogroms continued

cultural effects:

- creolization in destination countries
- spread of foods (italian -> us pizza, indian -> caribbean roti)
- spread of english as global language
