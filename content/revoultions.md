revolutionary theory:
	crane briton wrote the anatomy of revolution (1938)
	says modern revolutions tend to follow 4 stages:
		1. citizens become dissatisfied with the government
		2. moderates gain more power
		3. radicals take over in a "terror" phase
		4. cycle completes in period of relative calm + acceptance ("thermidorean reaction" -- named after a month in french revolutionary calendar)

causes:
	rise of nationalism: sense of unity between people and not necessarily the government
	development of ideas in the [[enlightenment]]
	resentment of taxation + colonial control
	new elites (creoles, bourgeoisie) wanted political power matching their wealth

ideologies:
	power to govern was in the hands of the people
	people have the right to influence government
	liberalism: economic and political ideology emphasizing protection of civil rights
	nationalism: loyalty to nation rather than monarch
	socialism + marxism (later in unit)

major revolutions:

american revolution (1775-1783):
	[[americas]] colonies gained their own identity
	britain lost a bunch of money in french and indian war and made america pay for it (stamp act, tea act)
	"no taxation without representation"
	declaration of independence 1776 -- thomas jefferson, lifted from john locke
	america revolts
	this is THE template for everything after

french revolution (1789-1799):
	[[europe]] -- people overthrew king louis xvi
	deeper causes:
		increasing inequality among 3 estates -- common people (3rd estate) paid ALL the taxes
			1st estate = clergy
			2nd estate = nobles
			3rd estate = everyone else
		increasing national debt from failed economic reforms
		increasing food prices -- basic goods soaring
	louis xvi calls estates-general 1789 -- 1st + 2nd outvote 3rd
	3rd estate leaves -> demands NATIONAL ASSEMBLY -- "TENNIS COURT OATH" (taken at indoor tennis court)
	july 14, 1789 -- storming of the BASTILLE (most famous french prison) -- THE symbol of the revolution
	national assembly issues:
		declaration of the rights of man + citizen (similar principles to US declaration of independence)
		civil constitution of the clergy -- catholic church loses special privileges, placed under state control
		makes church an OPPONENT of revolution
	women focus anger on marie antoinette + her extravagant lifestyle
	1791 new constitution -- france becomes limited monarchy
	louis tries to escape with family -> captured -> placed under house arrest
	louis is now a "traitor to the revolution"
	jacobins take over -> 1792 monarchy abolished, france becomes REPUBLIC
	jan 1793 -- louis xvi publicly executed
	REIGN OF TERROR begins -- this is where the revolution comes off the rails
		maximilien robespierre + 12-member committee of public safety = judge, jury, executioner
		guillotine to quickly execute anyone speaking against the revolution
		olympe de gouges executed -- author of declaration of the rights of women + the female citizen
	the bloodshed convinces many to step back -> moderate government returns
	people want order -> [[napoleon]] emerges as a leader
	napoleon takes over, spreads enlightenment ideas across europe by conquest
	napoleonic code = lasting legal influence
	(see [[napoleon]] for full age of napoleon detail)

haitian revolution (1791-1804):
	haiti (saint-domingue) -- french sugar colony, brutal slavery
	enslaved africans revolted
	toussaint louverture led, jean-jacques dessalines finished
	beat french, british, AND spanish armies
	first independent black republic
	first + only successful slave revolt to become a country
	scared every other slave-holding power into cracking down harder

latin american revolutions (1810s-1820s):
	creoles vs spanish peninsulares (casta system frustration)
	simon bolivar -- "the liberator", freed venezuela, colombia, ecuador, peru, bolivia
	jose de san martin -- argentina, chile, peru
	miguel hidalgo -- mexico (started it, executed)
	jose maria morelos -- continued mexican struggle
	dom pedro -- brazil, peaceful independence from portugal 1822

unification movements (nationalism):
	italy 1871 -- giuseppe garibaldi (red shirts), camillo cavour (politician), king victor emmanuel ii
	germany 1871 -- otto von bismarck, "blood and iron", franco-prussian war united german states
	these flipped nationalism from liberatory to aggressive (sets up wwi)

women's rights:
	seneca falls 1848 -- elizabeth cady stanton, declaration of sentiments
	mary wollstonecraft (earlier, 1792)
	women's suffrage movements grow

failed revolutions of 1848 -- across europe, mostly crushed but spread liberal ideas

after [[napoleon]]'s fall -- congress of vienna (1814-15):
	klemens von metternich (austria's conservative pm) leads
	goal: reestablish former borders + dynasties pre-napoleon
	stress controlling all nationalist movements + restore "balance of power"
	creates 3 european camps:
		conservatives -- support royal family return, oppose revolutionary ideas
		liberals -- support government based on written constitutions + natural rights
		nationalists -- support people of common heritage forming separate homelands (often violent)
