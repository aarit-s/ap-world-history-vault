[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]
1945-1991 -- us (capitalism + democracy) vs ussr (communism + authoritarian)
NEVER direct war between them -- entire conflict mediated through nukes, proxies, espionage, propaganda

why "cold":
	[[mutually assured destruction]] -- both sides had enough nukes to end civilization, so direct war = suicide
	all the actual fighting happened through PROXIES (other countries) instead

structure of the world:
	bipolar world -- everything organized around us vs ussr axis
	NATO (1949) -- us + western europe military alliance
	WARSAW PACT (1955) -- ussr + eastern europe military alliance
	IRON CURTAIN -- churchill's term, the political/military divide across europe
	non-aligned movement (india, yugoslavia, egypt, etc) tried to stay out of it

major events / flashpoints:
	[[marshall plan]] 1948 -- us pumps $13 billion into western europe to rebuild + stop communism
	berlin airlift 1948-49 -- us flies supplies into west berlin when ussr blockades it
	korean war 1950-53 -- first hot proxy war, ends in stalemate at 38th parallel
	[[berlin wall]] 1961 -- physical wall divides east + west berlin
	[[cuban missile crisis]] 1962 -- closest the world ever came to nuclear war
	[[vietnam]] war 1955-75 -- us loses, north vietnam unifies under communism
	[[prague spring]] 1968 -- czechoslovakia tries reform, ussr crushes it
	soviet-afghan war 1979-89 -- ussr's vietnam, sets up taliban + al-qaeda later
	1989 -- berlin wall falls, eastern european communist govts collapse
	1991 -- soviet union DISSOLVES into 15 countries

other dimensions:
	SPACE RACE -- soviet sputnik 1957, us moon landing 1969
	arms race -- ICBMs, hydrogen bombs, submarines, espionage
	cultural war -- jazz, hollywood, blue jeans vs soviet propaganda
	red scare in us -- mccarthyism, hollywood blacklists

end of cold war:
	mikhail GORBACHEV (ussr leader) tries reform -- GLASNOST (openness) + PERESTROIKA (restructuring)
	reforms expose how broken the soviet system is -> backfire
	1989 revolutions across eastern europe (peaceful except romania)
	ussr dissolves dec 1991 -> us = lone superpower, sets up [[unit 9]]

big picture:
	defined the global atmosphere of FEAR from 1950s-1991
	shaped [[decolonization]] -- new countries had to pick a side or stay non-aligned
	ended european dominance, locked in us-ussr bipolar world
	left behind nuclear arsenals that still exist today
