[[1750-1900]]
britain vs [[qing]] [[china]]
THE moment china stops being a great power for a hundred years

setup:
	britain wanted chinese tea/silk/porcelain SO bad
	china wanted nothing from britain (self-sufficient)
	britain hemorrhaging silver paying for chinese stuff
	britain's solution: grow opium in [[india]], sell it to china
	chinese got addicted, silver flowed out of china
	qing govt tried to stop opium trade -> burned british opium

first opium war (1839-1842):
	britain wins easily (steamships + better guns)
	treaty of nanjing:
		china pays huge indemnity
		opens 5 treaty ports
		hong kong to britain
		extraterritoriality -- british in china dont follow chinese law
	first of the "unequal treaties"

second opium war (1856-1860):
	britain + france this time
	china loses again
	more ports opened, opium legalized, foreigners can travel inland
	russia takes a chunk of land

aftermath:
	"century of humiliation" begins
	[[self strengthening]] tries to modernize but fails
	leads to [[boxer rebellion]]
	leads to qing collapse 1911

big picture:
	classic example of economic [[imperialism]] -- britain didnt take over china, just controlled its economy
