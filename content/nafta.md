NORTH AMERICAN FREE TRADE AGREEMENT -- signed 1992, took effect jan 1994  
between us, canada, mexico  
president clinton signed it (negotiated under bush sr)

what it did:

- eliminated most TARIFFS between the 3 countries
- created free trade zone for goods, services, investment
- one of the largest free trade blocs in the world at the time
- set framework for cross-border investment + intellectual property protection

economic results:

- trade between the 3 countries TRIPLED within 20 years
- us manufacturing jobs moved to mexico (cheaper labor) -- factories called "MAQUILADORAS" along us-mexico border
- rust belt states (michigan, ohio, pennsylvania) hit hard -- losing factory jobs
- mexico's agriculture suffered -- us-subsidized corn flooded mexican market, displaced small farmers
- canadian economy got tighter integration with us
- massive growth in us-mexico cross-border supply chains (especially auto industry)

political backlash:

- 1994 ZAPATISTA UPRISING -- indigenous mexicans in chiapas revolted on the day NAFTA took effect (saw it as neoliberal exploitation)
- huge issue in us politics -- ross perot famously warned of a "giant sucking sound" of jobs going south
- contributed to 2016 trump campaign + general anti-globalization populism
- 2018 -- trump renegotiated as USMCA (basically nafta with minor changes)

big picture:

- textbook example of NEOLIBERAL GLOBALIZATION in [[unit 9]]
- tied to broader 1990s wave -- WTO 1995, EU expansion, china joins WTO 2001
- winners + losers: consumers + multinationals won, factory workers lost
- connects to bigger AP themes: economic interdependence, labor migration, anti-globalization movements
- parallels EU economic integration in europe + ASEAN in southeast asia
