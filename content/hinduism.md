ancient religion of [[india]]
varies a lot region to region + group to group -- "lack of structure" is partly why it's argued to be polytheistic
has auspicious days but not specific weekly/monthly prayer days like [[christianity]] / [[islam]] / [[judaism]]
polytheistic but with one underlying universal spirit (brahman)
caste system -- rigid social hierarchy you're born into
	brahmin (priests) > kshatriya (warriors) > vaishya (merchants) > shudra (workers) > dalits (untouchables)
	point of emphasis: caste system is argued to play a major role in social stability of [[india]] -- it organizes society
	you can't change caste in this life, only by reincarnation
karma + reincarnation -- your actions determine your next life
	goal: moksha (release from cycle of rebirth)

bhakti movement ([[1200-1450]]):
	devotion to just one of the hindu gods
	mounted challenge to social hierarchies already present (caste)
	open to women + lower castes -- you didnt need a brahmin priest
	mirabai -- famous female bhakti poet
	parallel to sufism in [[islam]] -- both emphasized personal relationship with god

later:
	[[sikhism]] developed partly from bhakti + [[islam]] [[syncretism]]
	[[mughal]] ruled over hindu majority -- sometimes tolerantly (akbar) sometimes not (aurangzeb)
