believing in ONE god (vs polytheism = many gods)

the big 3 monotheistic religions (all from the same region + share a common ancestor in abraham -- "abrahamic religions"):

- [[judaism]] -- oldest, ~2000 BCE
- [[christianity]] -- ~30 CE, builds on judaism, jesus as messiah
- [[islam]] -- 7th c CE, builds on both, muhammad as final prophet

why monotheism mattered historically:

- easier to use as a state-building tool -- one god + one ruler = one truth
- enables UNIVERSAL claims (god is for everyone, not just one tribe)
- fueled missionary expansion -- you can convert people, unlike most polytheistic religions
- created sharper conflict with other religions (only one can be right)

other examples of monotheism (less central to AP but still around):

- zoroastrianism (persia, predates the big 3, influenced all of them)
- sikhism ([[sikhism]]) -- founded in punjab, blends [[hinduism]] + [[islam]] influences
- bahai (1844, persia) -- modern syncretic religion

contrast with polytheism (covered separately):

- [[hinduism]] -- many deities, but some interpretations argue they're all aspects of one brahman
- [[daoism]] -- not really about gods at all, more philosophical
- indigenous traditions -- africa, americas, polynesia, etc -- usually polytheistic + nature-focused
