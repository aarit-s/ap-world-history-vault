east asian philosophy (NOT religion -- doesnt deal with afterlife)  
founded by confucius at end of zhou dynasty (~550 bce)  
contemporary with [[daoism]]

core ideas:

- society is hierarchical -- everyone has a place
- in order to achieve harmony: those below listen to above, those above listen to below
- huge emphasis on role of elders
- filial piety -- always do something that would make your elders proud
- 5 key relationships: ruler-subject, father-son, husband-wife, elder-younger, friend-friend
- all about RELATIONSHIPS + DUTY, not personal salvation

women under confucianism:

- subordinates
- stripped of legal rights
- limited access to education
- [[china]] song dynasty added foot binding as a status symbol

political implications:

- supports strong centralized government
- supports civil service examination -- merit through study, not birth
- scholar-gentry class came from this -- educated elites who staffed the buerocracy

neo-confucianism (song dynasty):

- championed by ZHU XI (1130-1200) -- the scholar who systematized the whole revival
- confucianism + buddhist philosophical ideas mixed in (took the metaphysics, dropped the religious authority)
- tried to push back against [[buddhism]] influence in [[china]]
- became dominant ideology for centuries
- spread to [[korea]] (yangban scholar-officer class), [[japan]], [[vietnam]]

modern relevance:

- still influential in east asian politics + business culture
- "confucian work ethic" framing
