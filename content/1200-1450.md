covers [[unit 1]] + [[unit 2]]  
the "post-classical" / medieval world

big shifts driving the period:

- POPULATION BOOM -- agricultural innovations doubled global pop
  - [[china]] -- champa rice (double-cropping)
  - [[europe]] -- three-field crop rotation system
- TECH DIFFUSION -- compass, paper money, gunpowder spread from east asia -> middle east -> europe
  - reshaped warfare AND administration
  - moved along [[silk roads]] + [[indian ocean trade]] + [[mongols]] networks
- state-building everywhere -- bureaucracies, taxation, religion-as-legitimacy

the major civs at this time:

- [[china]] -- song dynasty, technological + economic powerhouse
- [[japan]] -- borrowed from china but went its own way
- dar al-[[islam]] -- abbasid -> seljuk -> mamluk, stretches morocco to indonesia
- [[india]] -- delhi sultanate (muslim) ruling over hindu majority
- [[southeast asia]] -- khmer, srivijaya, majapahit
- [[americas]] -- aztec + inca + mississippian
- [[africa]] -- [[mali]], great zimbabwe, [[ethiopia]], swahili coast
- [[europe]] -- decentralized, feudal, the slums frankly

end of the period:

- [[mongols]] empire fragments
- [[bubonic plague]] devastates eurasia
- [[europe]] starts maritime exploration
- [[ottoman]] takes constantinople 1453 -> next era
