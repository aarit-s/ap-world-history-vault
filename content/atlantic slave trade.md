[[1450-1750]]
trans-atlantic slave trade -- THE defining demographic event of unit 4
12 million africans forcibly shipped to [[americas]]
~2 million died on the way

the triangle:
	europe -> africa: guns, textiles, manufactured goods
	africa -> americas: enslaved people (middle passage)
	americas -> europe: sugar, tobacco, cotton, silver

middle passage:
	the atlantic crossing
	chained below decks for weeks
	disease, starvation, suicide
	mortality 15-25%

why slavery in americas:
	indigenous population destroyed by [[columbian exchange]] disease
	plantations (sugar, tobacco, cotton, rice) need huge labor
	chattel slavery: race-based, hereditary, you and your kids are property forever

effects on [[africa]]:
	gender imbalance -- men taken in higher numbers
	rise of polygyny (one man, multiple wives)
	some african kingdoms got rich selling captives (asante, dahomey, kongo)
	other regions destabilized by slave raiding
	population stagnated for ~300 years

effects in [[americas]]:
	majority population in caribbean and brazil became african
	creole languages (mixing african + european)
	[[syncretism]] -- vodun, santeria, candomble (african religions + catholicism)

resistance:
	maroon communities -- escaped slaves who set up free societies in jungles/mountains
		palmares (brazil) -- lasted almost a century
		jamaica maroons -- forced british to sign a treaty
	revolts -- stono rebellion (1739), nat turner (1831)
	[[haiti]]an revolution -- ONLY successful slave revolt that became a country

abolition timeline:
	britain ends slave trade 1807, slavery 1833
	us emancipation 1865
	brazil 1888 (last in americas)
	then -> [[migration]] of indian/chinese indentured servants replaced slave labor
