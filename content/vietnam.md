[[1200-1450]]  
THE only east asian society to significantly resist [[china]] cultural dominance  
critical for comparative essays -- vietnam vs korea/[[japan]] in response to chinese influence is a common SAQ

key differences from other east asian societies:

- maintained village autonomy
- practiced nuclear families (vs extended families like in china)
- allowed greater role for women than [[confucianism]] societies
- adopted china's bureaucracy + examination system PARTIALLY (selective borrowing)
- scholar-officials remained connected to peasant communities (not aloof)

military resistance:

- guerrilla warfare against china
- near end of tang dynasty
- classic case of cultural borrowing without political submission

[[1450-1750]]  
remained independent for most of this period  
nguyen + trinh families compete for power

[[1750-1900]]  
french colonization -- becomes part of french indochina (with laos + cambodia)  
catholic missions (vietnam has the largest catholic population in se asia today)

[[1900-present]]  
ho chi minh + vietnamese independence movements  
vietnam war (cold war proxy) eventually wins independence
