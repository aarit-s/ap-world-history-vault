born in [[india]] (note: prior line said "united states" which seems like a typo) -- overtaken by [[hinduism]] in india but spread across asia
origin story:
	siddhartha gautama -- prince in the kshatriya (warrior) caste of [[hinduism]]
	leaves his life of luxury looking for "enlightenment"
	this is itself a BREAK from the constraints of the [[hinduism]] caste system
	attains enlightenment under the bodhi tree -> becomes "the buddha" (the awakened one)
	founds another religion that rejects strict caste hierarchy
Centered on 4 noble truths: 
	1. life is suffering
	2. we suffer because we crave
	3. we cease suffering when we cease craving
	4. in order to cease craving, follow the 8 fold path
Similar to [[hinduism]]:
	cycle of birth and death and reincarnation
	goal: to disolve into onnenes of the universe (nirvana)
Multiple denominations:
	Thervana (theravada): Sri Lanka + mainland southeast asia, monastic purity, personal enlightenment, regular people are too occupied to reach Nirvana
	Mayahana (mahayana): tibet + china + korea + japan -- BODHISATTVA ideal -- enlightened beings stay in the world to help others reach nirvana too
spread:
	[[silk roads]] -> [[china]] (where it merged with [[daoism]] -> chan/zen)
	then [[korea]], [[japan]] (zen)
	[[southeast asia]] -- mainland mostly buddhist
	classic [[syncretism]] case study -- adapted everywhere it went
