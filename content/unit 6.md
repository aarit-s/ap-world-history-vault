[[1750-1900]]  
consequences of [[industrial revolution]]  
basically once [[europe]] industrialized they had so much excess goods + need for raw materials that they went and took over everyone

state expansion -- [[imperialism]]

- [[scramble for africa]] -- carved up by 1900, 90% of africa under european control
- [[india]] -- british raj after [[sepoy mutiny]]
- [[china]] -- not formally colonized but carved into spheres of influence after [[opium wars]]
- settler colonies: south africa, australia, new zealand, algeria
- economic imperialism: u dont need to take over the country if u control its economy
  - [[opium wars]] forced china to open ports
  - [[ottoman]] capitulations gave europeans tariff exemptions

indigenous responses (CB LOVES THIS):

- rebellion:
  - [[sepoy mutiny]] (1857) -- india
  - [[boxer rebellion]] (1899-1901) -- china
  - ghost dance -- native americans
  - xhosa cattle killing -- south africa
  - samory toure -- west africa fought french
  - yaa asantewaa -- asante queen mother fought british
- reform/modernization:
  - [[tanzimat]] -- ottoman defensive modernization
  - [[self strengthening]] -- qing china "chinese learning, western tech"
  - [[meiji restoration]] -- japan went FULL western and pulled it off
- new states:
  - sokoto caliphate -- west africa
  - zulu kingdom -- southern africa
  - muhammad ali -- modernized egypt

global economy:

- free trade > mercantilism (adam smith won)
- industrialized core vs non-industrial periphery
- export economies: latin america sells one cash crop (bananas, coffee, sugar) to europe
  - monoculture made these countries fragile
- suez canal (1869) -- cut shipping time europe<->asia
- panama canal (1914) -- atlantic<->pacific
- transnational businesses (united fruit, hbc, voc 2.0)

technology of empire:

- steamships (go upriver into africa, used to be impossible)
- quinine (malaria treatment, makes africa survivable for europeans)
- maxim gun (early machine gun, european militaries way overpowered)
- telegraph + railroads (control the colonies fast)

[[migration]] (other half of unit 6):

- push factors: famine ([[ireland]] potato), persecution (jewish pogroms), poverty
- pull factors: jobs in industrial cities, cheap land, gold rushes
- new patterns:
  - europeans -> americas (huge)
  - chinese + indian indentured servants -> caribbean, africa, southeast asia
    - coolie trade replaced slavery after abolition
  - irish -> us, britain
  - italian -> americas
- consequences:
  - ethnic enclaves (chinatowns, little italys)
  - gender imbalance (men migrated alone)
  - nativism: chinese exclusion act (us 1882), white australia policy
  - remittances back home

ideologies of empire:

- social darwinism (already in [[imperialism]])
- white mans burden (kipling poem) -- "duty" to civilize
- civilizing mission (france mission civilisatrice)
- scientific racism

[[nationalism]] flips:

- in europe -> unification (germany 1871, italy 1871)
- in colonies -> anti-colonial nationalism brewing (sets up unit 7)
  - indian national congress founded 1885
