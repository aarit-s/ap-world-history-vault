[[1200-1450]]  
sinified -- experienced deep [[china]] cultural influence  
became tributary state during silla kingdom  
capital modeled after chang'an  
elites adopted [[confucianism]] + [[buddhism]]

key difference from china (CB asks comparison questions about this):

- maintained bone-rank system -- HEREDITARY social hierarchy
- did NOT implement merit-based civil service exams
- so korea = born into status, china = tested into status

writing systems:

- elites used chinese characters
- hangul (15th century) -- new phonetic alphabet, accessible to common people
- huge literacy implications

[[1450-1750]]  
yi (joseon) dynasty rules  
neo-confucian state  
[[japan]] invaded under hideyoshi 1592 -- devastating, korea defended with admiral yi sun-sin's "turtle ships"  
became increasingly isolationist (the "hermit kingdom")

[[1750-1900]]  
[[japan]] colonizes korea 1910 after [[meiji restoration]]  
brutal colonial rule -- forced japanese language, names, comfort women system  
sets up post-wwii partition + korean war
