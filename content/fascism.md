[[1900-present]] / [[interwar period]]
totalitarian, ultra-nationalist, anti-communist political ideology
emerges in italy + germany after [[wwi]] + great depression
key features:
	all economic activity under state control
	focus on the STATE rather than the individual
	flag-waving + bowing to leader (cult of personality)
	scapegoats minorities
	uses paramilitary force + secret police
	authoritarian/dictatorial

MUSSOLINI in ITALY:
	benito mussolini organized the FASCIST PARTY
	pledged to return italy to greatness (post-wwi disappointment)
	used "BLACK SHIRTS" as muscle to smash opposition
		secret police / enforcement force
	1922 -- "MARCH ON ROME"
		italian king gives all power to mussolini
	brought all economic activity under state control
	authoritarian system
	allies with germany (axis powers in wwii)
	overthrown + executed 1945

HITLER in GERMANY:
	adolf hitler -- austrian-born, served in [[wwi]]
	first taste of fame: book MEIN KAMPF ("my struggle/life") written in prison
		blamed jews + other groups for germany losing wwi
		talks about idea of master ARYAN race
	attracts more followers as great depression hits germany hard
		unemployment + prices skyrocket due to depression + [[treaty of versailles]] reparations
	NAZI PARTY (national socialist) promises:
		create jobs
		end reparations to other countries
		rearm german military
		(the last 2 directly violate [[treaty of versailles]])
	1933 -- hitler nominated CHANCELLOR of germany
	soon after: dissolves democracy, becomes FUHRER (absolute leader)
	policies:
		enabling act -- gives him dictatorial power
		nuremberg laws 1935 -- strips jews of citizenship
		kristallnacht 1938 -- coordinated attack on jewish businesses + synagogues
		rearmament (violates versailles)
		annexes austria 1938 (anschluss), takes czechoslovakia 1938-39
	invades poland sept 1939 -> WWII begins
	the holocaust -- systematic murder of 6 million jews + millions of roma, disabled, gays, political prisoners
	suicide 1945 in berlin bunker

why fascism rose:
	[[treaty of versailles]] humiliation (germany)
	great depression destroying livelihoods
	weak weimar republic + weak italian government
	fear of communism after [[russian revolution]]
	[[nationalism]] taken to extreme
	people wanted strong leadership in chaos

vs [[stalin]]'s soviet communism:
	both totalitarian, both have secret police, both use propaganda + terror
	difference: fascism = ultra-nationalist + capitalist-friendly
	communism = international workers' revolution + state-owned economy
	but in practice -- hard to tell them apart from the inside
