babur -- founder, won battle of panipat 1526
	descendant of timur + genghis khan (turkic-mongol lineage)

akbar (r. 1556-1605) -- THE GREAT mughal:
	rules for nearly 50 years
	strengthens central government
	uses zamindars: regional officials paid with land (not salaries), collected taxes from peasants
	religious tolerance:
		ABOLISHED jizya (tax on non-muslims)
		supports [[sikhism]]
		marries hindu wives
		encourages trade + culture
	social reforms:
		bans child marriage
		bans sati (widows burning themselves on funeral pyres)

shah jahan:
	builds the TAJ MAHAL after wife's death
	peak mughal architecture -- blends islamic + hindu artistic styles
	expensive building projects strain finances

aurangzeb (last great mughal, late 1600s):
	ENDS religious tolerance
	suppresses hindu practices
	bans music
	persecutes [[sikhism]] -- sikhs militarize in response
	expands empire militarily but drains treasury
	accelerates decline

zamindar: elite landowners were granted authority to tax peasants on behalf of governments
decline:
	heavy taxation on peasants -> revolts
	zamindars keep more tax money + create private armies
	loyalty shifts away from emperor
	increasing british + french influence in [[india]]
	sets stage for european imperial control -> [[sepoy mutiny]] -> british raj
