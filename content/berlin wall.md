[[1900-present]] / [[unit 8]]
1961-1989 -- physical wall separating east berlin (soviet-controlled) from west berlin (us/uk/french-controlled)
155 km long, surrounded all of west berlin

context:
	post-[[wwii]] germany was split into 4 occupation zones -> eventually east germany (ussr) + west germany (us+uk+france)
	berlin was DEEP inside east germany but also split 4 ways -- west berlin was a capitalist island in communist territory
	huge problem for ussr: east germans kept fleeing to west via berlin (millions left between 1949-1961)
	"brain drain" -- losing all the educated people to the west

why it went up:
	1961 -- east german government (with ussr backing) builds the wall basically overnight
	stop the bleeding of citizens to the west
	became THE physical symbol of the [[cold war]] divide

life with the wall:
	families literally split overnight
	guards shot people trying to cross (~140 confirmed deaths)
	"checkpoint charlie" was the famous us-soviet crossing point
	west berlin = neon, jazz, freedom propaganda; east berlin = gray, surveillance, stasi secret police

why it fell:
	1989 -- gorbachev's reforms ([[cold war]] glasnost + perestroika) signal ussr won't crush eastern european uprisings anymore
	mass protests sweep eastern europe
	nov 9 1989 -- east german government accidentally announces open borders early -> crowds rush the wall, guards stand down
	germans on both sides physically tear it apart
	germany officially reunifies 1990

big picture:
	the symbol everyone uses for the start AND end of the [[cold war]] in europe
	"tear down this wall" -- reagan's famous 1987 speech at brandenburg gate
	hits all 6 AP themes -- politics, economics, culture, technology, society, environment
	when it fell, the soviet bloc collapsed within 2 years
