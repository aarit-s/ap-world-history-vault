[[1750-1900]] (rules ~1799-1815)
napoleon bonaparte -- emerges after the french [[revoultions]] reign of terror
extremely successful general -> claims title "emperor of the french" 1804

his quote: "since one must take sides, one might as well choose the side that is victorious, the side that devastates, loots, and burns. considering the alternative, is it better to eat than be eaten."
	tells us he was a dominant + strong leader (strong ≠ good)

why he was so popular:
	NAPOLEONIC CODE -- equal rights for all male citizens under the law
		right to trial by jury
		freedom of religion
		(no guaranteed rights for women though)
	made peace with the pope
	more equal tax system
	french legion of honor -- meritocracy for govt + military positions
	BUT: massive spy network watching the people, tightly controlled media

his empire:
	won basically every battle for years -> vast french empire
	controlled most of [[europe]] either directly or through puppet states
	spread [[enlightenment]] ideas (and the napoleonic code) by conquest
	created international embargo of trade with britain = the CONTINENTAL SYSTEM

how he fell:
	1812 -- attempts to invade [[russia]]
	russia uses "scorched earth policy" + brutal winter
	napoleon loses ~500,000 men
	1813 -- abdicates throne, exiled to elba
	1815 -- escapes elba, returns to france to retake europe
	BATTLE OF WATERLOO -- duke of wellington defeats napoleon
		"meeting your waterloo" = your final defeat
	napoleon exiled to st helena (south atlantic island), dies alone there

aftermath -- congress of vienna (1814-15):
	leaders of [[europe]] meet to fix the napoleonic mess
	klemens von metternich (austria's conservative pm) leads
	goal: reestablish former borders + dynasties before napoleon
	stress controlling all NATIONALIST movements + restore "balance of power"
	creates 3 european camps:
		conservatives -- support royal family return, oppose revolutionary ideas, use troops to crush opposition
		liberals -- support government based on written constitutions + natural rights
		nationalists -- support people of common heritage forming separate homelands (often violent)
	the conservatism succeeds short-term but bottled-up nationalism EXPLODES later
	1848 revolutions, italian + german unification, eventually [[wwi]]

big picture:
	napoleon = the bridge between [[revoultions]] and the rest of [[1750-1900]]
	spread enlightenment ideas + nationalism even though he was a dictator
	napoleonic code is still the legal foundation in much of europe + latin america
