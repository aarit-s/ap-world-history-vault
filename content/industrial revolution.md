**The social classes** (CRITICAL — this was your SAQ 4 problem):

- **Bourgeoisie** — middle class, factory owners, capitalists
- **Proletariat** — industrial working class, factory workers
- These are THE terms the College Board uses. You can't write about industrialization without them.

**Marxism** (the other half of SAQ 4):

- Karl Marx + Friedrich Engels wrote the **Communist Manifesto in 1848**
- Argued proletariat should overthrow bourgeoisie
- This is one of the most-tested things in Unit 5. It directly responds to the new elites.

**The 4 ideologies** (memorize these by name):

- Liberalism (free markets, individual rights — Adam Smith fits here)
- Nationalism (loyalty to nation-state — fueled Italian/German unification)
- Socialism (workers should control production)
- Marxism/Communism (Marx's specific version)

**Reform movements** you missed:

- **Tanzimat reforms** [[ottoman]](Ottoman, 1839–1876) — defensive modernization
- **Self-Strengthening Movement** [[china]] (Qing China) — "Chinese learning, Western technology"
- These pair with Meiji as the "how non-Western states responded to industrialization" trio. Often tested together.

**Labor response:**

- **Trade unions / labor unions** organized workers
- **Abolition of slavery** (British 1807/1833, US 1865, Brazil 1888) — connected to industrial labor needs
