[[1750-1900]]
the carving up of [[africa]] by europeans, mostly 1880-1914
went from ~10% european-controlled in 1870 to ~90% by 1914

why now:
	[[industrial revolution]] needed raw materials (rubber, palm oil, copper, diamonds, gold)
	quinine made interior of africa survivable for europeans
	steamships could go upriver
	maxim guns gave europeans massive military advantage
	nationalism -- european powers competing for prestige

berlin conference (1884-85):
	called by bismarck (germany)
	14 european countries divided africa AT A TABLE
	zero african representatives invited
	rules:
		you claim a coastal area, you get the interior behind it
		you have to "effectively occupy" to keep it
	drew dumb borders that ignored ethnic groups, kingdoms, languages
		caused civil wars + ethnic conflict for the next 150 years

who took what:
	britain -- egypt, sudan, south africa, kenya, nigeria, "cape to cairo" goal
	france -- west + north africa (algeria, morocco, senegal, mali region)
	belgium -- congo (leopold ii personally owned it -- atrocities on a genocidal scale)
	germany -- tanganyika, namibia, cameroon
	portugal -- angola, mozambique
	italy -- libya, eritrea, somalia
	only ethiopia + liberia stayed independent
		ethiopia beat italy at battle of adwa 1896 (rare W)

african resistance:
	zulu kingdom -- shaka zulu, beat british at isandlwana
	samory toure -- west africa, 15 years vs french
	yaa asantewaa -- asante queen mother, war vs british 1900
	ethiopia -- only one that fully succeeded

big picture:
	pairs with [[opium wars]] / [[sepoy mutiny]] -- the [[imperialism]] complex of [[unit 6]]
	consequences set up unit 7+8 (decolonization, modern african borders)
