[[1450-1750]] (italian starts ~1400, northern ~1500)  
cultural rebirth in [[europe]] inspired by classical greek + roman ideas  
emphasized humanism, realism, and individual achievement  
centered first in italian city-states like florence, then spread north

italian renaissance:

- leonardo da vinci -- mona lisa, the last supper, notebooks (art + anatomy + engineering + science genius)
- michelangelo -- david, la pieta, sistine chapel ceiling
- raphael -- combined classical balance with religious themes, madonna paintings
- patronage by wealthy families like the medici in florence

northern renaissance:

- more religious focus + more practical / merchant-class flavor
- johann gutenberg -- printing press, first complete bible printed 1455
  - MASSIVE consequence: people no longer rely solely on priests for biblical interpretation
  - books cheaper, literacy increases, ideas spread rapidly across europe
  - this directly enables the [[reformation]]
- erasmus -- humanist scholar
- jan van eyck -- oil painting techniques

why it happened in italy first:

- [[crusades]] brought back classical knowledge via [[islam]] world
- wealthy italian city-states from mediterranean trade
- [[bubonic plague]] aftermath -- labor shortage + disillusionment with church -> shift in worldview
- access to roman ruins + texts (italy literally sat on top of roman history)

what it set up:

- [[reformation]] (literacy + questioning authority)
- scientific revolution
- [[enlightenment]]
- age of exploration
